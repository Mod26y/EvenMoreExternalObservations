Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/fortibleed-credential-theft-campaign-linked-to-lynx-ransomware/

1) The FortiBleed credential theft campaign is linked to the INC and Lynx ransomware operations. Researchers discovered a server containing credentials stolen from over 73,000 Fortinet devices. The campaign involved a custom packet-sniffing tool called "FortiGate Sniffer," which intercepted VPN credentials from compromised FortiGate firewalls. Further investigation tied the campaign to ransomware groups through a Windows server connected to FortiBleed infrastructure. The campaign is larger than initially thought, affecting over 430,000 devices worldwide and deploying sniffers on 19,000 devices, exploiting a possible Nextcloud zero-day vulnerability, and using persistent backdoor accounts.

2) This attack is significant because it potentially sets the stage for widespread ransomware attacks on organizations using Fortinet devices, which are common in different sectors. The exploitation of a large number of devices and the vast scale of stolen credentials highlight vulnerabilities in network infrastructure that can lead to severe security breaches. Identifying links with ransomware groups suggests the potential use of these credentials for further targeted attacks, stressing the interconnected nature of modern cyber threats and the necessity for improved security posture.

3) Organizations should immediately assess and strengthen their network security controls, particularly those using Fortinet devices. It is advisable to perform a comprehensive review of device configurations, apply available patches, and monitor for indicators of compromise, such as unauthorized access and use of suspicious accounts like "adminin." Regularly update and simulate breach detection mechanisms to prevent unauthorized access and credential-theft attempts. Staying informed on threat intelligence updates, like the upcoming technical white paper from SOCRadar, can enhance cybersecurity defenses against dynamic and evolving threats.
