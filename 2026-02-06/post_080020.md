Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/ransomware-gang-uses-ispsystem-vms-for-stealthy-payload-delivery/

**What happened:**  
Cybercriminals are leveraging ISPsystem's VMmanager, a legitimate virtualization management platform, to host and deliver ransomware payloads using virtual machines (VMs) with the same hostnames. Researchers from Sophos discovered these VMs in various ransomware operations, including LockBit, Conti, and more. This exploitation utilizes ISPsystem's design flaw, where default VM templates use identical hostnames and system identifiers, granting significant cover for malicious activities. This setup enables threat actors to blend harmful entities amid legitimate infrastructure, complicating efforts to identify and dismantle the malicious components efficiently.

**Why it matters:**  
This stealthy exploitation of ISPsystem's VMmanager by ransomware operators underlines a critical vulnerability in managing virtual infrastructure. The situation is concerning as it aids the proliferation of cyberattacks by making malicious activities harder to detect and disable due to the look-alike nature of the VMs used. This tactic effectively hides the attack infrastructure, increasing its resilience against takedown efforts and complicating attribution. As ransomware attacks are costly and damaging to organizations globally, identifying and mitigating such vulnerabilities can significantly reduce the risk and impact of these attacks.

**What actions should be taken:**  
Organizations should refine their monitoring and detection strategies to identify abnormal patterns in virtual machine naming and provisioning. Collaboration with hosting providers like ISPsystem to address VM template vulnerabilities is crucial. Developing and deploying unique hostnames and identifiers for VMs can prevent similar abuses. Additionally, engaging with ISPs and domains showing non-compliance in cybercrime reporting can pressure them to improve practices. Increase investment in threat intelligence to stay ahead of tactics used by such sophisticated attackers. Cybersecurity frameworks and response plans should include adaptive measures specifically for virtualization platform vulnerabilities and potential exploitation methods.
