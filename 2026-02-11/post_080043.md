Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/malicious-7-zip-site-distributes-installer-laced-with-proxy-tool/

A fake 7-Zip website is delivering a trojanized installer that contains a malicious payload, turning the victim's computer into a residential proxy node. The installer mimics the original website's design and includes an actual copy of 7-Zip alongside malicious files designed to exploit the host system. The malicious program alters firewall rules and profiles system capabilities to support the attacker's proxy network. It impacts a wide range of software, including other popular applications like TikTok and WhatsApp, exposing users to various risks through this increasingly prevalent cyber threat.

This incident is significant as it showcases advanced social engineering tactics and the potential for wide-scale cyber exploitation through commonly trusted software. The use of residential proxies originating from unsuspecting users complicates tracking malicious activities, such as phishing and credential stuffing. This style of attack highlights vulnerabilities in software supply chains and the importance of software source verification, emphasizing the challenge for defenders in monitoring and differentiating legitimate traffic given the sophisticated obfuscation techniques employed.

In response to this threat, organizations should emphasize verifying software sources before downloading and installing applications, especially from less-known URLs or third-party sites. Providing security awareness training can help users recognize trusted sites and download practices. Detecting such malware requires enhanced network monitoring to recognize anomalies indicating proxy activities. Implementing rigorous endpoint security measures, such as employing heuristic and behavior-based analysis, can further help detect and mitigate these threats. Regular audits of digital certificates in use and ensuring their validity can also prevent exploitation by similar tactics.
