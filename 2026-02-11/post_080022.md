The article discusses the emergence of the SSHStalker botnet, which exploits outdated Linux vulnerabilities and uses the IRC protocol for command-and-control operations. The botnet employs a traditional mechanism associated with older internet protocols to achieve reliability, emphasizing scalability and cost-effectiveness rather than stealth. By leveraging SSH brute force attacks and cron jobs, SSHStalker compromises cloud infrastructure, especially targeting Oracle Cloud. This botnet uses classic IRC mechanics for resilience, enforcing a worm-like propagation mechanism to extend its reach and performing activities like AWS key harvesting and cryptomining.

The importance of this development lies in the botnet's use of legacy C2 channels to build a resilient network while capitalizing on older, often overlooked vulnerabilities.
