Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/gentlemen-ransomware-uses-multiple-edr-killers-to-disable-defenses/

1) The Gentlemen ransomware group utilizes a ransomware-as-a-service (RaaS) model and has developed a series of Endpoint Detection and Response (EDR) killers, including a main tool known as GentleKiller. The tool exists in at least eight variants that disguise themselves as legitimate security products. These tools exploit the "bring your own vulnerable driver" (BYOVD) technique to gain elevated privileges and disable security mechanisms, ensuring that data theft or encryption processes proceed unchallenged. They specifically target over 400 processes linked to 48 different security vendors.

2) This development is significant because it indicates an increased sophistication in ransomware tactics, making detection and prevention even more challenging. By employing EDR killers, the attackers can bypass multiple layers of security, increasing the risk of successful attacks on organizations and exposing them to data breaches, financial loss, and operational disruptions. With the Gentlemen ransomware gang targeting critical infrastructures like a Romanian energy provider, there's a heightened concern for national security and critical public services.

3) To counter such sophisticated threats, organizations should implement a multi-layered security approach, focusing on both prevention and detection. Regular updates and patching of security software can help mitigate vulnerabilities utilized by the BYOVD technique. Advanced threat detection systems that monitor for anomalies in legitimate software behavior could improve detection. Additionally, conducting regular breach and attack simulations can help gauge the effectiveness of security protocols and adjust strategies accordingly. Engaging in proactive threat intelligence sharing across industries can further bolster defenses against such evolving threats.
