Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/klue-oauth-breach-linked-to-icarus-salesforce-data-theft-attacks/

**What Happened:**
Klue, a market intelligence platform, experienced a breach of OAuth tokens linked to their Battlecards integration, allowing a new extortion group known as "Icarus" to steal Salesforce CRM data from multiple organizations, including Huntress. This data extraction was facilitated by compromised OAuth credentials, using automated scripts to retrieve sensitive information through Salesforce APIs. The attackers first accessed Klue’s backend systems, then implemented malicious updates to siphon OAuth tokens, which were used to illegally access and extract data from associated Salesforce environments.

**Why It Matters:**
This incident underscores the vulnerabilities present in third-party integrations and the significant risks posed by OAuth token compromises. The attack not only led to sensitive data theft impacting several organizations but also demonstrated the sophistication and adaptability of new extortion groups like Icarus. Such breaches expose organizations to extortion demands, operational disruptions, and reputational damage. Understanding this attack highlights the importance of securing integrations and monitoring unauthorized access, presenting an opportunity for organizations to reassess the security measures around their third-party service connections.

**Actions to be Taken:**
Organizations using Klue integrations should promptly audit their Salesforce and associated SaaS logs for any unusual activity, particularly from the listed IPs. It’s advisable to revoke and rotate all OAuth tokens to prevent unauthorized access and terminate current active sessions. Reviewing Salesforce logs for atypical API usage can also help identify potentially compromised accounts. Additionally, temporarily disabling unnecessary third-party integrations and reinforcing security protocols can mitigate further vulnerabilities. Companies should also update their incident response plans based on this breach's findings to enhance preparedness against similar threats.
