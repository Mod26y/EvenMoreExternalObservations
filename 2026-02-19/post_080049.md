**What happened:**  
The fintech company Figure Technology Solutions experienced a data breach that compromised nearly 1 million accounts. The breach, attributed to the ShinyHunters extortion group, resulted from a social engineering attack where an employee was deceived into giving access. The leaked data include personal identifiers such as email addresses, names, phone numbers, physical addresses, and dates of birth. The breach was revealed after the hacked data appeared online in February 2026, with the company later confirming the attack.

**Why it matters:**  
This incident highlights vulnerabilities even within organizations leveraging advanced blockchain technology. The breach exemplifies the growing threat posed by social engineering, particularly in phishing campaigns that bypass traditional security measures by exploiting human error. The exposure of sensitive personal information could lead to identity theft and financial fraud, potentially impacting customer trust and causing reputational damage to the company. It also underscores the need for enhanced employee awareness and robust security protocols against social engineering tactics.

**What actions should be taken:**  
Organizations could prioritize enhancing their cybersecurity posture through comprehensive security awareness and training programs targeting social engineering and phishing attacks. Implementing sophisticated multi-factor authentication beyond SMS or email may mitigate unauthorized access risks. Regularly testing and adapting incident response strategies to address social engineering threats could improve organizational resilience. Companies might also wish to conduct routine security audits and consider deploying advanced threat detection solutions to identify and prevent breaches promptly.
