Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/critical-infra-honeywell-cctvs-vulnerable-to-auth-bypass-flaw/

The article reports on a critical vulnerability discovered in Honeywell CCTV products, allowing attackers to bypass authentication. Identified as CVE-2026-1670, this flaw lets attackers change the recovery email for device accounts, potentially taking over accounts and gaining unauthorized access to camera feeds. This vulnerability affects multiple Honeywell camera models deployed in various critical and commercial settings. Despite no noted public exploitation, the exposure of critical infrastructure surveillance systems to unauthorized access poses significant security risks.

This issue is significant because Honeywell CCTV systems are widely used in sensitive and critical infrastructure environments. These cameras monitor and secure spaces such as offices, warehouses, and potentially critical facilities. Unauthorized access could lead to privacy invasion, compromised security, and potential operational disruptions. The critical nature of this vulnerability (scored at 9.8) highlights the urgency and potential impact on national security and business operations if exploited.

In response, organizations using these affected systems should reassess their CCTV network security. Reducing network exposure by placing devices behind firewalls provides added protection. Secure remote access should be ensured through up-to-date VPN solutions, limiting chances of unauthorized access. Engaging with Honeywell's support team for patch information and applying any updates once available will help mitigate risks associated with this vulnerability. Continuous monitoring and implementing strict access controls further prepare organizations against potential exploitation.
