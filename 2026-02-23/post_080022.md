Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/amazon-ai-assisted-hacker-breached-600-fortigate-firewalls-in-5-weeks/

1) A Russian-speaking hacker exploited AI tools to breach more than 600 Fortinet FortiGate firewalls over five weeks, targeting exposed management interfaces and weak credentials without multi-factor authentication (MFA). This campaign, which spanned 55 countries, employed generative AI to facilitate network access, automate reconnaissance, and craft scripts for lateral movement within compromised systems. Despite not targeting specific industries, the attack leveraged both common passwords and AI-produced tools, yielding considerable network reconnaissance data and a systematic approach to infiltrating further into victim networks.

2) This incident highlights a shift in the cybersecurity landscape where generative AI lowers the entry barrier for less-skilled hackers, enabling them to execute sophisticated attacks. The campaign underscores the critical importance of securing management interfaces and employing strong authentication methods like MFA. This case also serves as a warning of how AI services could be exploited to automate and amplify the reach of cyber threats, resulting in significant exposure for organizations that fail to patch vulnerabilities or implement robust security protocols.

3) Organizations should prioritize securing their firewalls and management interfaces by ensuring MFA is enabled and credentials are complex and unique. Regular audits to identify exposed services and the installation of patches on edge devices can prevent unauthorized access. Additionally, IT teams should monitor SSH activities and VPN account creation for anomalies, set up alerts for brute-force attacks, and invest in AI threat detection tools to identify AI-assisted compromise attempts. Training staff and maintaining an updated incident response plan can mitigate the impact of such threats.
