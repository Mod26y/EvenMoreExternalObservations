1) The article discusses the importance of cultivating a healthy security culture in organizations by encouraging employees to report potential risks. It suggests that fostering an environment where employees feel comfortable and responsible for identifying and reporting security threats leads to improved overall cybersecurity posture. The premise is that having multiple eyes and ears increases the likelihood of identifying risks early before they can be exploited by malicious actors.

2) This topic is important because human error and insider threats are two significant factors in cybersecurity incidents. When employees actively participate in reporting risks, organizations can address vulnerabilities more effectively and reduce the likelihood of a breach. The article highlights how valuable a proactive internal culture is in complementing technical security measures. This employee involvement not only enhances awareness but also helps organizations in threat prevention and mitigation strategies.

3) Organizations may consider implementing training programs that empower employees to recognize and report security threats without fear of negative consequences. Developing clear and accessible communication channels for reporting issues and rewarding proactive behavior could also be beneficial.
