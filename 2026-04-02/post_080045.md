Original Article: https://www.darkreading.com/remote-workforce/latam-cyber-talent-overlooked-cyberattack-glut

1) The article discusses the burgeoning pool of self-taught cybersecurity talent in Latin America that is being overlooked amid a surge in cyberattacks globally. With an increasing frequency of cyberattacks, the need for skilled cybersecurity professionals is more critical than ever. However, despite possessing valuable skills, self-taught individuals in Latin America face barriers in gaining visibility and opportunities in the global cybersecurity job market.

2) This issue matters because the cybersecurity skills gap is a pressing global challenge, with many regions, including the US, experiencing a shortage of skilled workers. By not tapping into overlooked talent pools like those in Latin America, organizations may miss out on valuable expertise that could mitigate the impacts of rising cyber threats. Furthermore, it highlights an opportunity for diversification and enrichment of the cybersecurity workforce.

3) Actions that could be taken include establishing partnerships between Latin American educational institutions and international cybersecurity organizations to promote talent. Companies should consider new hiring practices, recognizing skills through non-traditional educational paths. Additionally, supporting training programs in underrepresented regions could help pipeline talent to where it's most needed. Encouraging remote work and providing opportunities for skill development can help integrate this talent into the global workforce, fortifying defenses against cyber threats.
