Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/apple-expands-ios-18-updates-to-more-iphones-to-block-darksword-attacks/

1) What happened:

Apple expanded its iOS 18 updates to cover more iPhones, in response to the DarkSword exploit kit, which targeted devices running versions 18.4 to 18.7. Originally, updates were limited to older and select newer models, leaving a gap in protection for devices capable of supporting iOS 26. The DarkSword exploit kit, publicized by a researcher online, had been used widely by various threat groups, deploying malware such as GhostBlade and GhostKnife. The latest update, iOS 18.7.7, now brings critical security patches to a broader range of devices, enhancing protection against web attacks.

2) Why it matters:

This expansion in updates is crucial because it addresses a significant gap in security for iPhone users who haven't upgraded to the latest iOS version. The DarkSword exploit kit, being widely available on platforms like GitHub, increases the risk of attacks on iPhones, especially as it is used by various malicious entities, including international espionage groups. By providing support to more devices, Apple helps mitigate potential data breaches and protect users' sensitive information. This is especially vital as mobile devices are primary tools for personal and business communications, holding extensive personal data.

3) What actions should be taken:

Device users should ensure that they have Automatic Updates enabled to receive the latest iOS 18.7.7 update, which safeguards against the DarkSword vulnerabilities. Security teams in organizations should educate staff about the importance of updating their devices promptly to minimize exposure to these risks. Additionally, IT departments should monitor devices for any unusual activity that may indicate exploitation attempts. For enhanced security, a review of device upgrade policies might be beneficial, encouraging users to adopt the latest supported iOS versions where feasible to maintain robust protection.
