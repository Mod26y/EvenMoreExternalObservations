Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/teampcp-deploys-iran-targeted-wiper-in-kubernetes-attacks/

1) What happened: The hacking group TeamPCP has launched a campaign targeting Kubernetes clusters with a script that deploys a destructive payload specifically targeting systems configured for Iran. The payload wipes all systems identified as Iranian using timezone and locale checks. On systems that are not Iranian, the malware instead installs the CanisterWorm backdoor, which was used in previous attacks, to maintain access. TeamPCP exploits Kubernetes-native features for lateral movement and has been reported to use alternative methods like SSH for spreading, especially when Kubernetes is absent.

2) Why it matters: This campaign underscores an escalation of geopolitically motivated cyberattacks, highlighting the severity of destructive malware threats targeting specific regions. The use of Kubernetes clusters represents a focus on cloud-native environments, indicating that these systems are increasingly being targeted due to their widespread adoption. The case demonstrates how attackers can exploit these technologies for both precision-targeted attacks and broader persistent threats, compelling organizations to consider geographical and political factors in their threat landscapes.

3) What actions should be taken: Organizations utilizing Kubernetes clusters should enhance monitoring and alerting for unusual patterns, especially around DaemonSets and privileged containers. Implementing robust defenses against brute force and unauthorized SSH access could prevent further lateral movement if attacked. Regularly updating and auditing software for supply-chain vulnerabilities, such as dependency exposures used in CanisterWorm, would be advisable. Furthermore, fostering awareness of geopolitical cyber threats could help prepare organizations for possible targeted attacks, encouraging them to adopt region-specific safeguards.
