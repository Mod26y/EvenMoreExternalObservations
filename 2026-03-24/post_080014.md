Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/artificial-intelligence/openai-rolls-out-chatgpt-library-to-store-your-personal-files/

**1) What happened:**

OpenAI introduced a new feature called 'Library' for ChatGPT, enabling users to store personal files or images on OpenAI's cloud storage. This feature is available to Plus, Pro, and Business users globally, except in the European Economic Area, Switzerland, and the UK. Files uploaded through ChatGPT are saved automatically in a secure location for easy future access, reinforcing ChatGPT's usability as a document repository. Users have control over stored files — they remain until manually deleted and require selecting and deleting from the Library. OpenAI removes these files from its servers within 30 days post-deletion.

**2) Why it matters:**

This development enhances ChatGPT's functionality by allowing users to store and manage files in the cloud. However, it raises critical privacy and security concerns, especially given that files are stored until manually deleted. Organizations and individuals must consider potential data exposure risks, as data retention beyond its utility could lead to data breaches or unauthorized access. The legal and compliance aspects, particularly the GDPR restrictions in Europe, highlight the importance of understanding regional regulations. This feature may improve user convenience but necessitates careful consideration of data governance policies.

**3) What actions should be taken:**

Organizations using ChatGPT Library should assess their data governance strategies to encompass the new feature, including regular audits and staff training on securely managing and deleting data in the cloud. Privacy policies and consent forms should be updated to reflect the storage aspects of this feature. Additionally, organizations operating in sensitive environments or regions with stringent data privacy laws should perform risk assessments and possibly restrict the use of the Library feature, pending further clarity on OpenAI’s data retention and deletion practices. Continuous monitoring and adopting robust access controls can further mitigate associated risks.
