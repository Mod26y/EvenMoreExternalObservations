Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/new-infratrust-report-reveals-infrastructure-flaws-admins-should-patch-first/

1) The InfraTrust Pulse report, introduced by Eclypsium, offers a prioritized list of infrastructure vulnerabilities to patch, focusing on exploitability, exposure, and real-world risk rather than traditional CVSS severity scores. The inaugural July 2026 report tracked 61 advisories from 14 vendors, highlighting critical flaws being actively exploited by state-sponsored hackers, especially targeting network edge devices. Specific advisories, such as those for SonicWall SMA1000 and Fortinet FortiSandbox, were highlighted due to active exploitation.

2) This report is crucial because it shifts the prioritization for patching from theoretical severity to practical considerations like exploitability and exposure, aiding in the immediate mitigation of vulnerabilities that pose real-world threats. As attacks against infrastructure from nation-state actors increase, focusing on internet-exposable and already-exploited flaws can significantly enhance an organization's defensive posture, mitigating the risk of major breaches that could impact critical infrastructure and telecommunications.

3) Organizations should review the InfraTrust Pulse report to identify and prioritize patching of vulnerabilities that pose the greatest risk, particularly those actively exploited and affecting internet-exposed systems. This involves updating systems from key vendors like SonicWall, Fortinet, and others noted for current vulnerabilities. Additionally, augmenting patch management strategies to incorporate exploitability as a primary criterion and leveraging tools for regular assessment and monitoring of vulnerabilities can enhance defense mechanisms against state-sponsored and other advanced attacks.
