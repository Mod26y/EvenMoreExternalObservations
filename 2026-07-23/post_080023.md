Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/swiss-rail-giant-stadler-rejects-123m-ransom-demand-after-cyberattack/

1) In a recent cyberattack, Swiss rail manufacturer Stadler Rail declined to pay a $12.3 million ransom demanded by the Everest ransomware group. The attack involved the infiltration of a shared data exchange platform with a supplier, but Stadler reports no significant IT system or operational impacts. The company emphasized that only non-critical technical data was stolen, and operations remain unaffected globally.

2) This incident highlights the growing trend of ransomware groups focusing on data theft and extortion rather than encrypting data. Stadler's firm stance against paying the ransom underscores the importance of not succumbing to extortion, which could otherwise incentivize further criminal activity. The focus on shared platforms also emphasizes the need for robust security protocols with all third-party associates.

3) Organizations should consider a multi-layered security strategy that includes monitoring third-party data exchanges and implementing robust data access controls. Regular assessments and breach simulations could help find vulnerabilities that might go unnoticed. Encouraging a strong security culture that includes not yielding to extortion could deter attackers over time. Collaborating and communicating transparently with law enforcement could further assist in tackling cybercrime and reducing its impact.
