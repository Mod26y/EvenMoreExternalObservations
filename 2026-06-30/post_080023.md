**What happened:** WhatsApp introduced a new feature allowing users to reserve usernames, providing an additional layer of privacy by hiding phone numbers from non-contacts. Users can select usernames which act as identifiers, thereby protecting their phone numbers from exposure. This feature includes an optional key requirement for initiating contact through usernames, rolling out gradually over the next few months. To secure a username, users can pre-register via the app settings. Some usernames are reserved for government, public figures, and businesses. This move comes in response to growing privacy concerns and aligns WhatsApp with other messaging platforms offering similar features.

**Why it matters:** The introduction of usernames on WhatsApp represents a significant enhancement in user privacy, allowing individuals to share contact details without exposing their phone numbers. This feature addresses privacy concerns where personal information could be vulnerable to unauthorized access or misuse. By offering increased control over who can contact users, WhatsApp is enhancing user safety, potentially reducing spam and targeting by third parties. This development aligns with broader trends in digital communication prioritizing user privacy and data protection, making it a notable point for organizations to consider in their communication strategies.

**What actions should be taken:** Organizations and users should update to the latest version of WhatsApp to access the username reservation feature. Encourage stakeholders to select unique usernames, enhancing privacy by limiting phone number exposure. Consider informing employees and user groups about these changes, highlighting the benefits of username use for increased privacy and security. Additionally, integrating this feature into your organization's mobile communication policies may be beneficial, tailoring communication guidance to emphasize responsible username management and the importance of privacy in digital communication platforms.
