Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/nissan-discloses-employee-data-breach-linked-to-oracle-zero-day-attacks/

**1. What Happened:** Nissan disclosed that it suffered from a data breach after threat actors, identified as the ShinyHunters group, exploited a zero-day vulnerability in Oracle PeopleSoft systems. This breach impacted numerous organizations, with Nissan being a specific target. The attackers accessed sensitive personal information of current and former employees across the United States, Canada, Mexico, and Brazil, including details such as Social Security numbers and banking information.

**2. Why It Matters:** This breach is significant as it highlights vulnerabilities in widely-used software systems, specifically Oracle PeopleSoft, which many organizations rely on for crucial operations like payroll and employee management. The breach threatens the privacy and security of sensitive employee information, potentially leading to identity theft and financial fraud. It underscores the importance of addressing zero-day vulnerabilities promptly and stresses the need for robust incident response strategies to protect organizational data and limit damage in the event of a security breach.

**3. Actions to be Taken:** Organizations using Oracle PeopleSoft should evaluate and apply Oracle's emergency mitigations and patches to safeguard against similar attacks. It is advisable to enhance system monitoring for unusual activity, deploy additional identity verification measures, and restrict sensitive operations to secure networks. Additionally, offering credit and dark web monitoring services to affected individuals can help mitigate potential fallout. Cybersecurity training and awareness campaigns for employees can also bolster an organization’s defense against such breaches. Engaging with cybersecurity experts to conduct vulnerability assessments and penetration testing could provide insights into other potential risks.
