Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/cisa-warns-of-akira-ransomware-linux-encryptor-targeting-nutanix-vms/

1) The Akira ransomware group has expanded its targeting capabilities by encrypting Nutanix AHV virtual machine disk files. Originally affecting VMware ESXi and Hyper-V environments, Akira now also exploits the SonicWall vulnerability CVE-2024-40766. Nutanix's AHV platform, widely used to manage virtual machines, is now facing these attacks. The ransomware attempts to encrypt .qcow2 files, a virtual disk format for Nutanix AHV, marking a shift in Akira's attack strategies initially observed by cybersecurity agencies in November 2025.

2) This expansion in Akira’s targeting is crucial because it signifies an evolution in ransomware tactics that could affect a large number of organizations using Nutanix’s infrastructure. Virtual machines on platforms like Nutanix AHV are integral to many corporate IT environments, meaning their compromise could lead to significant operational disruptions. By exploiting widely used vulnerabilities and targeting backup solutions, Akira enhances its chances of executing successful ransomware attacks, thus increasing risks for critical data loss and financial damages.

3) Organizations should enhance their cybersecurity measures by promptly updating and patching all systems, focusing particularly on known vulnerabilities such as those identified in SonicWall and Veeam Backup & Replication servers. Regular offline backups and the implementation of enforced multifactor authentication could mitigate the impact of ransomware attacks. Conducting regular security audits, training staff on secure credential practices, and using robust access controls would also fortify businesses against Akira's expanded attack capabilities. Organizational resilience can be further supported by updated intrusion detection systems to quickly identify any suspicious activity.
