Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/new-prinz-eugen-ransomware-prioritizes-recent-files-for-encryption/

**1) What happened:**

The article discusses the emergence of a new ransomware operation, Prinz Eugen, which prioritizes recently modified files for encryption, aiming to maximize damage by targeting files likely to be business-critical. Notably, this ransomware does not leave a ransom note on the system, reducing forensic artifacts and complicating detection. Prinz Eugen hackers employ legitimate remote monitoring tools and usually gain access via stolen RDP credentials. The group does not operate under a ransomware-as-a-service model and currently manages operations independently with evidence of encryption and data exfiltration for multiple known victims.

**2) Why it matters:**

The emergence of Prinz Eugen ransomware represents a shift in ransomware tactics, prioritizing critical files and eliminating ransom communication artifacts to evade detection and pressure organizations to comply with demands. This new tactic highlights a threat evolution that organizations need to prepare for, emphasizing the importance of securing legitimate software tools and credentials. With its focus on recent files, Prinz Eugen can cause significant operational disruption and economic impact on organizations, thus increasing the urgency for comprehensive cybersecurity defenses and awareness regarding user access.

**3) What actions should be taken as a result of this information:**

Organizations should strengthen security protocols by ensuring robust protection of RDP credentials and implementing multi-factor authentication to limit unauthorized access. Regularly updating and monitoring network defenses, especially around remote management tools, is vital. Companies should evaluate and reinforce endpoint detection and response (EDR) solutions, incorporating indicators of compromise linked to Prinz Eugen to improve early detection. Simulating breach scenarios using tools like breach and attack simulations can enhance preparedness and refine incident response strategies. Continuous employee training on recognizing phishing and other access-laden threats will also reduce risk levels.
