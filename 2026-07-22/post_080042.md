1) The article titled "Ransomware Is Accelerating, But It's Not Because of AI" likely discusses the increasing frequency and severity of ransomware attacks. This escalation is probably attributed to factors other than artificial intelligence, possibly including more sophisticated hacking techniques, broader attack surfaces due to remote work, or increased availability of ransomware-as-a-service operations. Although AI might enhance some attack strategies, the article suggests that other dynamics are more significant in driving the current trends in ransomware incidents.

2) This increase in ransomware activity is critical because it highlights a growing threat to organizations' operational integrity and financial stability. Ransomware not only compromises sensitive information but also disrupts services, impacting public trust, especially within essential public services and infrastructures such as those operated by large counties. Understanding the true drivers of these trends, beyond just AI, allows for more focused and effective risk mitigation strategies and resource allocation.

3) Organizations would benefit from adopting comprehensive cybersecurity practices focused on both technology and human factors. This includes regular security training for employees, routine updates, and patch management for all systems. Investing in advanced threat detection tools that do not solely rely on AI can also enhance defenses. Additionally, maintaining comprehensive incident response plans and securing robust cyber insurance could mitigate potential impacts. By understanding and addressing the multiple facets driving ransomware's rise, counties can better protect themselves from this evolving threat.
