Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/whatsapp-phishing-attack-uses-fake-business-docs-to-hack-pcs/

1) What happened: A global malware campaign is exploiting WhatsApp, using compromised accounts to send deceptive messages that entice recipients to download malicious VBScript files. These files masquerade as business documents, tricking users into executing them. Once opened, the VBScript downloads additional scripts that disable Windows security measures and install the ManageEngine Endpoint Central software, allowing attackers remote access to the victim's system. This campaign has been observed in several countries, including Brazil, India, and the UK, indicating a broad scope of impact.

2) Why it matters: This phishing attack highlights the evolving threat landscape where everyday applications like WhatsApp are used as vectors for malware distribution. Targeting WhatsApp, a widely used communication platform, increases the potential scale and damage of the attack. By mimicking legitimate business documents, the campaign exploits users' trust, potentially impacting personal and corporate systems. The ability to gain remote access can lead to data breaches, financial loss, and the spread of further malware, emphasizing the need for heightened awareness and security measures.

3) What actions should be taken: Users should exercise caution when receiving files through WhatsApp, even from known contacts, and verify such files independently. Security software like antivirus programs should be kept up-to-date and used to scan any downloads before execution. Organizations should educate employees on recognizing phishing attempts and maintaining skepticism towards unsolicited digital content. Additionally, security teams should reinforce endpoint security, regularly test their systems for vulnerabilities, and review security protocols to prevent similar breaches from occurring within their environments.
