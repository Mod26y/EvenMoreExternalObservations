Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/legal/california-bans-data-broker-reselling-health-data-of-millions/

1) The state of California, through its Privacy Protection Agency, took action against the marketing firm Datamasters for illegally selling health and personal data of users without a proper registration as a data broker. The firm, now fined $45,000, reportedly bought and resold personal information of millions, including people with sensitive health conditions. The agency's final order mandates Datamasters cease transactions involving Californians' data and delete existing records by December's end. Future data involving Californians must be deleted within 24 hours of receipt, with compliance measures enforced for the next five years.

2) This event underscores the growing importance of data privacy regulations as they aim to protect individuals' sensitive information, particularly in healthcare. Such regulatory actions highlight the risks of data misuse, emphasizing the need for greater transparency and accountability amongst data brokers. By enforcing heavy penalties on non-compliant firms, this move strengthens consumer trust in privacy protection laws and potentially sets a precedent for other states or countries to take similar actions in safeguarding their citizens' data against unregulated exploitation.

3) Given these developments, organizations that collect or handle consumer data should review and update their compliance strategies regarding data brokerage. Staying abreast of regional privacy laws and ensuring timely registration can help mitigate legal and financial risks. Companies need to implement robust data governance policies and prioritize transparency in data transactions. Additionally, regular audits and staff training on data protection can enhance compliance and safeguard consumer trust. In preparing for submission to new compliance platforms like California's DROP, businesses can ensure smoother operations with customer privacy requests.
