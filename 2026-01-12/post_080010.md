Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/instagram-denies-breach-amid-claims-of-17-million-account-data-leak/

1) What happened: A media report emerged claiming that data from over 17 million Instagram accounts was leaked online, possibly from an unconfirmed 2024 API incident. The data allegedly includes usernames, email addresses, phone numbers, and more. Instagram, owned by Meta, denies any recent breach, attributing the claims to potentially older, scraped data from a 2022 incident without hard evidence. While Meta confirmed fixing a bug facilitating bulk password reset email requests, they reassured users that no new compromise of its systems occurred.

2) Why it matters: This situation underlines the ongoing challenges in safeguarding personal data against malicious actors, highlighting potential vulnerabilities in API interfaces. Although Instagram claims no new breach, the exposure of sensitive information such as email addresses and phone numbers increases the risk of phishing scams and social engineering attacks. The abundance of leaked data, even if outdated, can still aid cybercriminals in crafting targeted attacks, demonstrating the need for persistent vigilance and enhanced security measures to protect social media users.

3) What actions should be taken: Individuals should ignore any unsolicited Instagram password reset emails to avoid potential phishing scams. Enhancing account security settings is advisable, particularly enabling two-factor authentication to provide an additional defense layer. Organizations can take this opportunity to evaluate and strengthen their API security and data protection strategies. Regular audits and vulnerability assessments can help identify weaknesses before they're exploited. Public communication should be proactive, informing users about safeguarding measures against phishing and malicious attempts using leaked data.
