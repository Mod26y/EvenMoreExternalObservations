Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/bubble-ai-app-builder-abused-to-steal-microsoft-account-credentials/

1) Threat actors are exploiting the no-code app-building platform Bubble to create and host deceptive web applications that bypass phishing detection in campaigns aimed at compromising Microsoft accounts. By generating these apps on a legitimate and trusted domain such as Bubble, the malicious links are not flagged by email security systems, leading users to phishing pages mimicking Microsoft login portals and covertly stealing user credentials.

2) This development is significant as it highlights an evolving threat vector where legitimate platforms are exploited, making detection and prevention more challenging. The use of platforms like Bubble allows attackers to bypass security measures, potentially leading to unauthorized access to sensitive information associated with Microsoft 365 accounts. This tactic's propagation through phishing-as-a-service platforms suggests an imminent rise in sophisticated phishing schemes.

3) Organizations would benefit from enhancing their security awareness training, emphasizing this new threat vector and the risks of interacting with unfamiliar application platforms. Implementing advanced threat detection solutions that recognize patterns indicative of fraudulent domains or AI-generated sites could improve defenses. Collaborating with trusted app-building platforms to strengthen their sign-up and monitoring processes may also deter misuse by threat actors, ultimately safeguarding users and organizations alike.
