Original Article: https://www.darkreading.com/identity-access-management-security/more-attackers-logging-in-not-breaking-in

1) The article discusses a trend in cybersecurity where attackers are increasingly gaining unauthorized access to systems not by breaking in through exploits or vulnerabilities, but by logging in using stolen or compromised credentials. This shift emphasizes the growing risk of attacks that exploit weaknesses in identity and access management, such as phishing schemes, credential stuffing, and other social engineering tactics, which allow cybercriminals to masquerade as legitimate users.

2) This trend matters because it highlights a significant challenge for organizations in protecting their sensitive data and systems. Traditional security measures focused on keeping attackers out may no longer be sufficient if unauthorized access is gained through valid credentials. The increasing sophistication of credential-based attacks requires organizations to rethink their approach to authentication and access management. It underscores the importance of implementing robust identity verification and monitoring systems to detect and respond to unauthorized access attempts.

3) In light of this information, organizations should consider enhancing their identity and access management strategies. This could involve implementing multi-factor authentication to increase security complexity, conducting regular audits of user accounts and permissions, and employing continuous monitoring and anomaly detection techniques to identify suspicious login activities. Organizations could also focus on educating employees about the risks of phishing and social engineering, encouraging strong password policies, and ensuring that any stolen credentials are rendered ineffective through rapid deauthorization processes.
