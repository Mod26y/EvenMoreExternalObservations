Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/europe-sanctions-chinese-and-iranian-firms-for-cyberattacks/

1) The European Council recently sanctioned three companies, two Chinese and one Iranian, along with two individuals, due to cyberattacks targeting EU critical infrastructure. Integrity Technology Group supported the hacking of 65,000 devices across six EU states. Anxun Information Technology provided services targeting critical infrastructure, and its co-founders were involved in cyberattacks against EU states. Emennet Pasargad engaged in influence campaigns, advertising billboard hijacking, and data breaches, including offering personal information of Charlie Hebdo subscribers.

2) This matters as it highlights the increasing sophistication and coordination of state-linked cyber threats targeting critical infrastructures. With cyberattacks impacting national security, the EU's sanctions emphasize the importance of international collaboration in deterring such threats. Additionally, these actions underscore the need for robust cybersecurity frameworks and increased vigilance against these entities. The implications also extend to countering misinformation and safeguarding the integrity of public information, especially during major events like the Olympics.

3) Organizations should assess and strengthen their cybersecurity postures by enhancing monitoring capabilities for potential threats from sanctioned entities. Engaging in information-sharing initiatives with government agencies and industry peers could boost collective defense. Training employees in recognizing phishing and influence campaigns is beneficial. Additionally, reviewing third-party vendors to ensure no ties to blacklisted entities bolsters security. Encouraging collaboration with law enforcement and adhering to international cybersecurity regulations can further mitigate the risks associated with such malicious activities.
