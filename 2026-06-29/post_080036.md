Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/cisa-sets-urgent-deadline-to-fix-cisco-flaw-exploited-in-attacks/

**1) What happened:** The U.S. Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA) has identified a critical vulnerability, CVE-2026-20230, in the Cisco Unified Communications Manager Server. This server-side request forgery (SSRF) flaw is being actively exploited, prompting CISA to require federal agencies to patch it by June 28, as part of their obligations under Binding Operational Directive 26-04. Cisco released a patch on June 3, but recently, the vulnerability was observed being used in attacks to manipulate endpoints. Additionally, a critical flaw, CVE-2026-12569, in PTC's PLM products has been identified, also requiring urgent remediation by the same deadline.

**2) Why it matters:** This situation is critical because both vulnerabilities have severe implications for federal cybersecurity and potentially private sector entities using similar technology. The CVE-2026-20230 allows attackers to send specially crafted HTTP requests remotely and without authentication, posing risks of unauthorized access and manipulation. Similarly, the CVE-2026-12569 vulnerability in PTC's PLM software poses risks of remote code execution, affecting industries like manufacturing and retail. Effective and timely patching is vital to protect sensitive data and ensure the integrity of critical infrastructure.

**3) What actions should be taken:** It is important for all agencies and organizations using these affected systems to prioritize applying the necessary patches by the June 28 deadline. Secondary actions include conducting a thorough review of network security settings to identify any unauthorized access attempts and strengthening overall defenses by employing breach and attack simulation tests to ensure SIEM and EDR systems are effectively detecting potential threats. Organizations should also engage in comprehensive risk assessments and update incident response plans to mitigate potential vulnerabilities before they are exploited.
