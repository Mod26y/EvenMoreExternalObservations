Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/fbi-russian-hackers-now-target-signal-backup-recovery-keys/

1) What happened:
Recent reports by the FBI and CISA indicate that Russian hackers, linked to intelligence services, are executing a sophisticated phishing campaign targeting users of the messaging application, Signal. These phishing attempts focus on stealing Signal Backup Recovery Keys, thereby enabling attackers to access victims' historical messages. By masquerading as Signal support and suggesting users set up backups under the guise of a mandatory security update, the attackers prompt victims to provide their recovery keys, ultimately compromising sensitive data.

2) Why it matters:
This development highlights an evolution in phishing tactics aimed at breaching secure communication applications. Signal is widely used by individuals with high intelligence value, such as government officials, military personnel, and journalists, making it a critical threat vector. Compromise of Signal accounts through stolen recovery keys can lead to unauthorized access to sensitive communications, putting both personal privacy and national security at risk. It underscores the importance of vigilance against social engineering attacks, even when using applications with robust end-to-end encryption.

3) What actions should be taken as a result of this information:
Organizations and individuals should be vigilant about phishing attempts, especially those requesting sensitive information like recovery keys. Signal users should verify communications through official channels and avoid sharing backup recovery keys with anyone. Continuous user education regarding phishing tactics and social engineering is essential. Security policies should be updated to encourage generating new backup recovery keys if compromise is suspected. Reporting potential incidents to authorities like the FBI's Internet Crime Complaint Center (IC3) is crucial for tracking and mitigating these threats on a larger scale.
