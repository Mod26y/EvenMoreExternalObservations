Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/apple-fixes-ios-bug-that-retained-deleted-notification-data/

**What happened:** Apple discovered and addressed a vulnerability (CVE-2026-28950) in its iOS and iPadOS notification services, which allowed notifications designated for deletion to be retained on devices. This was addressed through a security update outside the regular cycle, although Apple has not clarified whether the flaw had been exploited maliciously. The issue came to light following reports of the FBI recovering Signal messages from a suspect's iPhone through notification data, highlighting potential privacy breaches.

**Why it matters:** The flaw's existence points to potential privacy risks, where deleted sensitive information could unintentionally remain accessible, thereby compromising user confidentiality and security. This incident underlines the importance of robust data handling practices and quick action when vulnerabilities are detected. For users of secure communication platforms like Signal, the retention of notification data constitutes a significant privacy risk, undermining the encryption assurances the application offers.

**What actions should be taken:** Devices should be promptly updated to the latest iOS and iPadOS versions (26.4.2 and 18.7.8) to mitigate risks tied to the notification data retention vulnerability. Moreover, users of privacy-focused messaging apps can further safeguard their data by configuring notification settings to minimize displayed content. Organizations should continuously educate users on cybersecurity best practices, including regularly updating devices and applications. Keeping abreast of security updates and promptly implementing them helps reinforce overall cyber hygiene.
