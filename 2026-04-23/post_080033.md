Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/spain-dismantles-major-47m-manga-piracy-platform-arrests-four/

1) The Spanish police have successfully dismantled a major Spanish-language manga piracy platform, operational since 2014. Serving millions globally, this platform offered unauthorized access to copyrighted manga, generating over $4.7 million from ad revenue, including potentially harmful pornographic ads viewed by minors. The investigation started in June 2025 and led to the arrest of four individuals, with one suspect in Almeria found developing a secondary site to potentially continue operations. Two concealed USBs containing cryptocurrency wallets valued at over $470,000 were seized.

2) This event highlights the substantial impact of online piracy on the cultural and publishing industries, demonstrating significant financial losses and intellectual property theft. The dismantling is crucial as it addresses piracy's promotional harm, particularly exposed to minors, thus protecting their online experience. Moreover, this case underscores the necessity for law enforcement to remain vigilant against evolving piracy tactics and safeguard copyrighted materials globally.

3) Actions suggested include strengthening collaboration between content creators and law enforcement to better detect and address piracy activities. Heightening awareness and tech support can help protect platforms from unauthorized content sharing. Additionally, improving age-verification mechanisms on online platforms can offer protection to minors. For organizations, routinely monitoring internet traffic for unauthorized content and engaging legal avenues for immediate cessation can be effective in curbing piracy.
