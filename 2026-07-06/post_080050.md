1) **What happened:** The article likely discusses a trend where larger Australian entities are experiencing a reduction in cybercrime risk as a result of improved security measures or shifting cybercriminal focus. However, this reduction in risk for larger entities is leading cybercriminals to target small-to-medium-sized businesses (SMBs) instead. SMBs may face increased cyber threats due to potentially having fewer resources and less robust cybersecurity practices compared to larger organizations.

2) **Why it matters:** This shift in cybercriminal focus toward SMBs underscores the need for heightened awareness and improved cybersecurity practices among smaller organizations. SMBs, often with limited resources, may find themselves more vulnerable to attacks, which can lead to significant financial and reputational damage. It highlights the necessity for SMBs to assess their current security protocols and ensure that they align with best practices to protect against the evolving threat landscape.

3) **What actions should be taken:** SMBs should conduct comprehensive risk assessments to identify potential vulnerabilities within their systems. Implementing cost-effective security measures such as regular employee training, strong access controls, and routine software updates can significantly enhance defense mechanisms. Collaborating with cybersecurity experts and considering managed security services could also be beneficial. Moreover, fostering information-sharing networks with other businesses can help SMBs stay informed about current threats and effective defense strategies.
