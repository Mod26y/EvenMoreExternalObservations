1) In this incident, cybercriminals used a tactic of impersonating Interpol to target small businesses with ransomware attacks. This social engineering method involves deceiving business owners into believing they are under legal scrutiny or investigation, thus prompting hasty compliance that leads to running malicious software. The attack plays on the authority and fear associated with Interpol to entice victims into downloading harmful programs disguised as legitimate files.

2) This development is significant because it highlights the evolving creativity in ransomware tactics, specifically targeting small businesses that may not have robust cybersecurity defenses in place. The masquerade as a reputable organization like Interpol increases the likelihood of success for the attackers by exploiting the trust and fear individuals have towards legitimate institutions. This underscores the need for heightened awareness and proactive measures in small business cybersecurity strategies.

3) Organizations can reduce their risk exposure by conducting regular staff training focused on recognizing and responding to social engineering and phishing attacks. Businesses should also ensure their cybersecurity policies are up-to-date, incorporate effective email filtering tools, and utilize endpoint protection systems. Continuous monitoring for suspicious activity and educating employees on the importance of verifying the legitimacy of unexpected communications, particularly those requesting sensitive actions, can further bolster defense against such tactics.
