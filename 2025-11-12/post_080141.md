1) What happened:
The article discusses the increasing role of military veterans in strengthening the cybersecurity sector. Veterans, equipped with unique skills and discipline acquired during their service, are filling crucial gaps within cybersecurity teams. This integration is driven by the growing demand for skilled professionals to combat sophisticated cyber threats. Veterans often bring a wealth of experience in handling intense situations, engineering problem-solving capacities, and demonstrating leadership under pressure, which are invaluable in cybersecurity roles.

2) Why it matters:
The inclusion of military veterans in cybersecurity is significant due to the persistent skills gap in the field and the continuously evolving cyber threat landscape. Veterans' unique attributes, such as their technical training, ability to work under pressure, and rigorous discipline, contribute to more resilient cybersecurity defenses. By leveraging their skills and experiences, organizations can potentially enhance their security posture, leading to strengthened national and organizational security, while simultaneously providing veterans with fulfilling post-military careers.
