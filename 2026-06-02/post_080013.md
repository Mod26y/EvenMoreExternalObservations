Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/hackers-hijack-thousands-of-sites-for-clickfix-and-fakeupdate-attacks/

**1) What happened:**

The DriveSurge group has been found distributing malware through two techniques, ClickFix and FakeUpdates, by compromising thousands of legitimate websites. These attacks redirect site visitors to malicious infrastructures using a Traffic Distribution System (TDS) called zTDS. The attackers trick users with fake technical issue resolutions or deceptive browser update prompts, leading them to inadvertently download malware. DriveSurge operates as an initial access broker, selling access on a pay-per-install model, thereby facilitating further attacks. Researchers identified technical patterns across compromised sites, indicating widespread and systematic exploitation.

**2) Why it matters:**

This large-scale hijacking of reputable websites for malware distribution highlights significant cybersecurity risks. As visitors to these sites unknowingly become targets, this increases concerns about the integrity of online data security and the potential for extensive financial and reputational damage. The campaign illustrates the persistent evolution of social engineering tactics, accelerating the need for stronger cybersecurity measures. Organizations and individuals are at higher risk of malware infections leading to data breaches, system compromise, or more sinister follow-on attacks, impacting operational integrity and privacy.

**3) What actions should be taken as a result of this information:**

Organizations should educate their users to emphasize downloading updates directly from verified sources within apps and to be cautious of unsolicited updates or commands. Implementing advanced monitoring tools capable of detecting unusual redirection patterns can help identify potential compromises early. Websites should regularly audit for vulnerabilities and unrecognized script injections, especially those resembling `t.js?site=<id>`. Investing in robust endpoint protection and intrusion detection systems also helps in mitigating such threats. Encouraging regular security training for staff fosters vigilance against such evolving cyber threats, reducing the likelihood of successful infiltration.
