1) The arrest of an individual by the Spanish National Police involved the unauthorized leaking of sensitive data from key state organizations like the National Cybersecurity Institute (INCIBE). This doxing operation targeted critical entities, compiling and disseminating personal data without breaching organizational systems. Instead, it utilized sources like older breaches and OSINT tools. The personal data of government personnel, including judges and prosecutors, was published online, posing national security concerns and individual privacy risks.

2) This incident underscores the severe repercussions of data leaks that can affect national security and individual safety. It highlights vulnerabilities in data management practices and the potential exploitation of even outdated information to create credible threats. Such events emphasize the necessity for robust data protection strategies and comprehensive incident response plans, as well as heightened awareness of data aggregation risks in critical government sectors.

3) In response to this incident, organizations should evaluate and strengthen their data protection policies, ensuring that even seemingly outdated information is securely managed. Implementing stringent access controls and continuous monitoring can prevent unauthorized data aggregation. Training staff on the importance of cybersecurity hygiene and the risks of public data sources can mitigate potential threats. Collaboration with legal and cybersecurity experts to simulate and respond to data leakage incidents can enhance incident preparedness and resilience.
