Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/multiple-london-councils-it-systems-disrupted-by-cyberattack/

1) What happened: The Royal Borough of Kensington and Chelsea and Westminster City Council in London experienced a cyberattack that disrupted multiple IT systems, including phone lines. This prompted these councils to activate emergency plans to ensure residents still received vital services. The outages particularly stem from shared IT infrastructure between these councils, with a third council, the London Borough of Hammersmith and Fulham, also taking precautions. The incident is identified as a ransomware attack affecting a service provider used by the councils, but no ransomware group has claimed responsibility yet.

2) Why it matters: The disruption of IT services in these councils is significant due to the critical services they provide to a combined population of 360,000 residents, including important national institutions and landmarks. This incident highlights the vulnerabilities in shared IT infrastructures and the broad impact of cyberattacks on public services. Such attacks lead to reputational damage, potential data privacy concerns, and hinder daily operations, underscoring the importance of robust cybersecurity measures and incident response strategies.

3) What actions should be taken: Organizations sharing IT infrastructures should strengthen their collaborative cybersecurity protocols, ensuring thorough penetration testing and regular audits to identify vulnerabilities. They should also establish comprehensive incident response plans involving all stakeholders and regularly train staff on these procedures. Increasing investments in cybersecurity technologies, like advanced threat detection and response systems, can limit the impact of potential breaches. Additionally, maintaining transparent communication with the public and stakeholders, as well as complying with data protection authorities, is crucial for trust and regulatory adherence.
