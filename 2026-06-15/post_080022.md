Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/ex-school-district-employee-jailed-for-hacks-on-former-employer/

This article outlines a case involving Ezekiel Dean Potter, a former IT support specialist for the Saydel Community School District, who was sentenced to 21 months in prison for launching cyberattacks against his previous employer. After being terminated, Potter retained access to the district’s systems, conducting attacks over a 21-month period. This resulted in significant disruptions to school operations, such as deleting social media accounts and educational access platforms, leading to account deletions and widespread chaos. His actions caused tens of thousands of dollars in damages, impacting the district's ability to function effectively.

The incident highlights the serious threat posed by insider knowledge and vulnerabilities in access control, emphasizing an urgent need for organizations to review and enhance cybersecurity protocols. Schools are particularly sensitive targets, as disruptions can impede student learning and protection of sensitive data. This case underlines how critical it is for organizations to ensure comprehensive offboarding procedures and maintain rigorous surveillance of account access even after employees have left, to mitigate potential retribution or nefarious actions.

In response to such incidents, organizations should implement robust offboarding procedures, ensuring immediate revocation of access credentials upon employee departure. Conducting regular audits and continuously monitoring account activity can help in detecting unauthorized access more swiftly. Furthermore, investing in breach and attack simulation tools can enable organizations to test and improve their defenses against potential internal and external threats. Engaging in continuous employee training on cybersecurity awareness and updating policies for incident response can further fortify defenses against similar security breaches.
