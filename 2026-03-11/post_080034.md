Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/new-beatbanker-android-malware-poses-as-starlink-app-to-hijack-devices/

**1) What happened:**  
The new BeatBanker malware, posing as a Starlink app, targets Android devices by pretending to be from the Google Play Store. It combines banking trojan capabilities with Monero cryptocurrency mining, enabling it to steal credentials and alter crypto transactions. Since its discovery in Brazil, BeatBanker has swapped its banking module for the BTMOB RAT, which provides attackers full control over infected devices. It uniquely maintains persistence using a near-inaudible MP3 file to keep its services running uninterruptedly, and uses modified XMRig miner to discreetly mine Monero based on device status.

**2) Why it matters:**  
BeatBanker presents a dual threat with capabilities extending beyond typical banking trojans by incorporating cryptocurrency mining, making it a more versatile and potent malware. Its ability to pose as legitimate apps and maintain persistence illustrates the increasing sophistication of mobile threats. This malware’s control over device functionality not only jeopardizes financial data but also affects device performance. The potential for its expansion beyond Brazil indicates a broader risk to Android users worldwide, underscoring the need for heightened security awareness and practices on mobile devices.

**3) What actions should be taken:**  
To mitigate risks from BeatBanker, users should avoid side-loading applications from outside the Google Play Store and scrutinize app permissions, especially those unrelated to app functionality. Regular Play Protect scans should be conducted to identify and remove malicious software. Organizations and security teams should educate users on distinguishing legitimate sources from fake ones and periodically review security measures to include protections against advanced threats like remote access trojans. Monitoring for unusual device behavior that could indicate malware activity will bolster defenses and help contain potential breaches early.
