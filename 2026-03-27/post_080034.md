Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/tiktok-for-business-accounts-targeted-in-new-phishing-campaign/

1) What happened:
A phishing campaign has been launched against TikTok for Business accounts, exploiting these accounts' high value for potential abuse in malvertising, ad fraud, and malware distribution. This campaign uses sophisticated techniques, like redirecting through legitimate Google Storage URLs and bypassing bot detection with Cloudflare Turnstile checks. Attackers lure victims to phishing pages hosted on domains masked as legitimate TikTok or Google Careers sites. These pages collect basic user information and serve reverse proxies that capture login credentials and session cookies, potentially bypassing two-factor authentication protections.

2) Why it matters:
This campaign highlights a new threat vector for TikTok for Business users and potentially compromises both TikTok and Google accounts via single sign-on features. With the ability to bypass two-factor authentication, the phishing campaign represents a significant escalation in threat actor capabilities, posing risks not only to personal data but also to company assets due to the accounts' perceived legitimacy and reach. The campaign underscores the ongoing challenge of safeguarding social media business platforms from creative phishing attempts and highlights the need for vigilant security practices among users.

3) What actions should be taken:
Organizations should train employees to recognize phishing exploits and emphasize the importance of verifying domains before entering credentials. Ensuring robust security measures like passkeys could add another layer of protection. Incident response teams should simulate phishing attacks to assess defensive readiness and improve organizational awareness. Companies should consider implementing additional security controls, such as monitoring for unusual login patterns and adopting zero-trust frameworks, to safeguard their accounts and information. Regular updates of security protocols in response to evolving threats are also advisable to ensure continued protection against account compromise.
