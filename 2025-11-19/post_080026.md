Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/microsoft/windows-11-gets-new-cloud-rebuild-point-in-time-restore-tools/

1) **What Happened:** Microsoft announced the introduction of two new recovery tools for Windows 11, Cloud Rebuild and Point-in-Time Restore (PITR), aimed at enhancing system recovery and reducing downtime from system failures or faulty updates. PITR allows users to restore their systems to a prior state, capturing complete system snapshots, while Cloud Rebuild enables a full OS reinstallation from the cloud. These features, part of the Windows Resiliency Initiative, will be integrated into Microsoft Intune in 2026, facilitating remote recovery actions across enterprise environments.

2) **Why It Matters:** These tools are important as they provide businesses and IT administrators with more robust and flexible options for managing and recovering from system failures, which are often costly in terms of both time and resources. PITR and Cloud Rebuild can significantly minimize downtime, thereby maintaining productivity and reducing operational disruption. Furthermore, their integration with Microsoft Intune reflects a shift towards centralized, remote management capabilities, streamlining IT operations and enhancing the resilience of enterprise systems against failure scenarios.

3) **What Actions Should Be Taken:** County IT departments could start preparing for the integration of these features by assessing current recovery protocols and configurations. Evaluating the existing organizational IT infrastructure's compatibility with Windows 11 can help streamline future adoption. Training staff on utilization of Microsoft Intune and familiarizing them with the principles of cloud-driven recovery processes would be beneficial. Additionally, exploring participation in the Windows 11 Insider preview program may provide early access to test these capabilities and develop best practices tailored to their specific environments.
