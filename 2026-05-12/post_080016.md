Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/official-checkmarx-jenkins-package-compromised-with-infostealer/

The article reports a security breach involving the Jenkins Application Security Testing (AST) plugin, which was compromised with an infostealer by TeamPCP, a hacker group known for conducting supply-chain attacks. This malicious version was uploaded to the Jenkins Marketplace after TeamPCP accessed Checkmarx's GitHub repositories using credentials stolen in a previous supply-chain attack. The rogue plugin includes credential-stealing malware, endangering any systems it was installed on.

This incident is significant due to its demonstration of the vulnerabilities inherent in software supply chains and the potential widespread impact on organizations using Jenkins, a popular CI/CD solution. Compromising a widely-used security plugin could lead to the exfiltration of sensitive information from numerous affected systems. It highlights the importance of diligent repository and credential management practices and the risks posed by insufficiently protected software development environments.

In response, organizations using the affected Jenkins AST plugin should immediately verify they are not using the rogue version and if necessary, revert to a version unaffected by the compromise. The investigation for potential impacts includes rotating all potentially compromised credentials and searching for signs of unauthorized access or persistent threats. Users should regularly check updates from Checkmarx and leverage published indicators of compromise to strengthen their defenses and prevent similar incidents. Additionally, ensuring strong secret management practices and securing development environments can mitigate future risks.
