Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/instructure-confirms-hackers-used-canvas-flaw-to-deface-portals/

In April 2023, hackers exploited multiple cross-site scripting (XSS) vulnerabilities in Instructure’s Canvas learning management system, allowing unauthorized access to modify login portals with extortion messages. This action aimed to pressurize Instructure into ransom negotiations, following a prior breach where attackers stole 3.6 terabytes of data. The attackers, identified as ShinyHunters, leveraged these vulnerabilities to gain access to authenticated admin sessions and deface portals, affecting countless educational institutions worldwide.

This incident highlights the critical need for robust cybersecurity practices in educational technology, as systems like Canvas handle significant amounts of sensitive data. The exploitation underscores the potential vulnerability of user-generated content features within online platforms, posing risks to academic institutions globally. The breach could undermine trust in digital education tools, disrupt academic operations, and compromise personal and educational records of millions.

Instructure should conduct a thorough security audit of Canvas, particularly focusing on user-generated content features to identify and patch vulnerabilities. Implementing more stringent security monitoring and response strategies, including frequent vulnerability testing and utilizing advanced security tools, would help detect threats proactively. Educating staff and users on recognizing and reporting suspicious activities is also essential to enhancing overall cybersecurity resilience.
