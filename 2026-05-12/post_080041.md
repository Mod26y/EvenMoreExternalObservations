1) The article “Tech Can't Stop These Threats — Your People Can” likely discusses the critical role employees play in mitigating cybersecurity threats that technology alone cannot combat. It highlights incidents where human factors, such as phishing attacks or social engineering, are exploited by cybercriminals. As the title suggests, technological defenses are not foolproof against these threats, and the human element becomes pivotal in recognizing and responding to potential security breaches effectively.

2) This issue matters because it underscores the limitations of relying solely on technological solutions for cybersecurity. While technology can address many vulnerabilities, human errors and manipulation are often exploited by attackers, leading to significant breaches. Emphasizing the human role in cybersecurity elevates awareness and responsibility, highlighting the need for continual education and training to ensure vigilance and preparedness against complex attack vectors that technology alone might not thwart.

3) As a result, organizations should focus on enhancing cybersecurity awareness among employees through regular training programs tailored to current threat landscapes. Simulating phishing attacks and offering workshops on recognizing social engineering tactics can strengthen the organization's security posture. Moreover, fostering a culture where employees feel responsible and are encouraged to report suspicious activities can significantly mitigate risks, complementing technological defenses and creating a more resilient organizational security framework.
