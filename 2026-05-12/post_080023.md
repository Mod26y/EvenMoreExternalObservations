Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/new-ghostlock-tool-abuses-windows-api-to-block-file-access/

**1) What happened:**

A new tool, GhostLock, was released, showcasing how attackers can misuse the legitimate Windows API function 'CreateFileW' to block access to files on local systems or SMB network shares. GhostLock abuses the 'dwShareMode' parameter to open files in exclusive mode, preventing other processes from accessing them. This approach acts as a denial-of-service attack, as it disrupts file access without requiring elevated privileges. The GhostLock tool can recursively lock numerous files, creating sharing violations until the attack is mitigated by terminating the processes or rebooting the affected systems.

**2) Why it matters:**

GhostLock introduces a novel way for attackers to disrupt operations by hijacking legitimate Windows operations. Unlike ransomware, this technique does not damage data but can cause significant operational downtime by barring access to critical files. Since it exploits legitimate file open requests, it may bypass conventional security measures designed to detect mass file writes or encryption attempts. As a distraction during larger attacks, GhostLock could overwhelm IT staff during data breaches or malware infiltration. The sophisticated nature of this method underscores the difficulties in detecting and mitigating lateral tools not producing typical threat indicators. 

**3) What actions should be taken:**

Organizations should enhance their defenses by implementing the shared detection rules and SIEM queries specified in the GhostLock whitepaper to detect a high count of open files with 'ShareAccess = 0'. This approach helps identify the attack at the storage platform management level. Regularly updating security measures and training staff to recognize signs of lateral movement can also mitigate such disruptions. Building robust incident response protocols, including immediate steps to terminate malicious sessions and reboot systems, will help resume normal operations swiftly. Additionally, collective threat intelligence sharing within the industry could provide more proactive defenses against such advanced tactics.
