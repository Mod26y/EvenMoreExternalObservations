Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/5-steps-to-managing-shadow-ai-tools-without-slowing-down-employees/

**1) What happened:**  
The article highlights the emergence of "shadow AI" tools within organizations, as employees use unapproved AI tools to enhance productivity. These tools often connect to sensitive company data through OAuth tokens or browser sessions, bypassing traditional security measures, with security teams lacking visibility. The article outlines steps to manage this trend safely, encouraging the adoption of formal AI governance policies and suggesting a more streamlined approach toward the approval and monitoring of AI tools used by employees.

**2) Why it matters:**  
This trend poses significant security risks, as unauthorized AI tools can access sensitive corporate data, potentially leading to data breaches or violations of compliance requirements. As AI tools become more prevalent, their use without oversight exacerbates the risk of data exposure and undermines established security controls. It is crucial for organizations to balance empowering employees with maintaining robust security measures, ensuring that AI tool usage does not compromise organizational security posture.

**3) What actions should be taken:**  
Organizations should conduct comprehensive inventories of all AI tools in use and develop a formal AI governance policy. This policy should encompass approved tools, data classification guidelines, and a process for requesting new tools. Businesses should implement continuous monitoring for AI tool usage to ensure visibility and evaluate a browser-native approach to balance security with usability. Streamlining the approval process for AI tools and providing real-time coaching and education will encourage safe AI tool use, allowing security teams to remain agile in managing emerging risks.
