Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/new-forg365-phishing-platform-uses-ai-to-target-microsoft-365-accounts/

The Forg365 PhaaS platform, utilizing AI, employs advanced phishing techniques to target Microsoft 365 accounts, combining adversary-in-the-middle (AiTM) and device code authentication methods. It generates phishing emails leveraging AI integration to duplicate legitimate services and evade detection. Notably, its use of a browser extension, "ForgCookie," provides continued access to compromised accounts by refreshing Microsoft SSO cookies for persistent account control.

This development underscores the increasing sophistication of phishing operations leveraging AI. It exemplifies how attackers continuously adapt to bypass security systems, exploiting trusted service channels, like Amazon SES and Cloudflare, enhancing scalability, and reach with reduced costs.

Organizations should consider reviewing security policies, focused on managing OAuth tokens and monitoring device-code authentications. Email security measures should be enhanced with AI-enabled threat detection tools. Disabling or monitoring device-code authentication, alongside regular account activity reviews for anomalous entries, could provide early detection and mitigation against similar threats.
