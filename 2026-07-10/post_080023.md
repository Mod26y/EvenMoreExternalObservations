Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/new-helix-vishing-group-emerges-in-sharepoint-data-theft-attacks/

1) The Helix vishing group has emerged, utilizing identity-focused tactics like vishing, device code phishing, and MFA abuse to steal data from SharePoint environments. They impersonate authority figures to gain trust, leading to device-code phishing schemes that grant them unauthorized access. Once inside, they exploit SharePoint, registering new multi-factor authenticator apps and exfiltrating files. This data is then used for extortion—threatening to release or selling it. ReliaQuest researchers note Helix's distinct SharePoint exploitation techniques and suggest links to prior groups ShinyHunters and BlackFile.

2) This matter as it highlights evolving and sophisticated social engineering tactics targeting organizations' cloud-based services, emphasizing the vulnerability of SharePoint. With businesses increasingly relying on SharePoint for collaboration, these attacks could lead to significant data breaches and financial losses if data is ransomed or sold. The potential connection to previous groups suggests a persistent threat actor that has adapted and continues to exploit advanced techniques to monetize stolen data, underscoring the need for robust security protocols.

3) Organizations should disable device code authentication to minimize vulnerability to Helix's tactics. Restricting SharePoint access to only managed devices adds another security layer. Companies should also be wary of new domain registrations and implement detection measures for known Helix tactics. It is beneficial to conduct regular breach and attack simulations to test and refine existing security systems, ensuring robust detection and response capabilities. Additionally, employee training on recognizing phishing and social engineering attempts can help mitigate the risk of falling prey to such schemes.
