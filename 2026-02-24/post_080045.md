Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/when-identity-isnt-the-weak-link-access-still-is/

**What happened:**

The article discusses the evolving challenges in workforce security where traditional identity-based access models are inadequate. In the modern work environment, employees access corporate resources from multiple locations and devices, making identity verification alone insufficient for ensuring security. As device conditions and contexts vary, the risk associated with granting access based solely on identity increases. Attackers exploit these gaps by misusing trust, such as stealing session tokens or using compromised devices, while the device risk landscape continually changes post-authentication. This scenario reveals the shortcomings of relying heavily on identity without considering device and contextual security signals.

**Why it matters:**

This situation matters because it exposes vulnerabilities in current cybersecurity approaches that can lead to data breaches. Over-reliance on identity as a sole criterion for access control fails to account for device security and contextual factors, leaving organizations at risk of exploitation. With stolen credentials involved in nearly half of all breaches, updating access models to include continuous device verification and real-time risk assessment is critical. These outdated systems hamper the ability to manage modern threats effectively and result in increased exposure to attacks, making it essential to evolve security frameworks to protect organizational assets.

**What actions should be taken:**

Organizations should adopt continuous access verification mechanisms that extend beyond identity checks to include device-based access controls. This encompasses verifying both user identities and device trustworthiness continuously, even during active sessions. Implementing solutions that enable dynamic security policies and offer user-friendly remediation options can reduce reliance on static access controls. Additionally, incorporating measures such as limiting the number and types of devices per user and enforcing proportionate security responses without disrupting workflows can mitigate risks. Adopting a comprehensive Zero Trust model that integrates identity and device signals holistically will strengthen security defenses against modern cyber threats.
