Original Article: https://www.darkreading.com/application-security/storm-brews-critical-no-click-telegram-flaw

1) **What Happened:** A critical vulnerability was identified in the Telegram messaging platform that allows for a no-click attack, meaning the victim does not need to interact, such as clicking or downloading, for the exploit to be executed. This flaw potentially enables attackers to gain unauthorized access to user data and manipulate devices remotely, making it a significant security threat given Telegram's expansive user base.

2) **Why It Matters:** The no-click nature of this flaw heightens its risk profile, as it can be exploited without user interaction, making it stealthy and harder to detect. Since Telegram is widely used for both personal communications and, increasingly, sensitive business transactions, such vulnerabilities can lead to widespread data breaches and espionage incidents, compromising both personal privacy and organizational security.

3) **What Actions Should Be Taken:** Organizations should conduct a risk assessment to determine the impact of this vulnerability on their business processes. Encouraging users to update to the latest Telegram versions promptly will mitigate this risk if a patch is available. Additionally, educating users about potential risks of using communication apps with known vulnerabilities and implementing more robust monitoring solutions are prudent steps until assurances of a fix are confirmed.
