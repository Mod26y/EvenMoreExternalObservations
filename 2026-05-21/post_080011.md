Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/microsoft-warns-of-new-defender-zero-days-exploited-in-attacks/

1) Microsoft has identified and released patches for two zero-day vulnerabilities in its Defender security suite, which have been actively exploited in attacks. The first vulnerability, CVE-2026-41091, allows privilege escalation due to improper link resolution, providing SYSTEM privileges. The second, CVE-2026-45498, can trigger denial-of-service states in systems running older versions of the Microsoft Defender Antimalware Platform. Security patches have been issued to mitigate these flaws, and efforts to ensure automatic updates are in place are encouraged.

2) Addressing these vulnerabilities is crucial because zero-day exploits can bypass existing security measures, granting attackers high-level access or disrupting services. Such breaches can lead to data theft, system downtime, and compromise of sensitive information. The involvement of CISA indicates the severity of the potential impact on government and private systems alike, emphasizing the risks posed by unpatched systems and the necessity of maintaining current defenses against evolving threats.

3) Organizations should verify that automatic updates are enabled for Microsoft Defender, ensuring they have the latest security patches. It's recommended to confirm the update installation by checking the Antimalware ClientVersion in the Windows Security application. Additionally, raising staff awareness about phishing and suspicious activities aids in mitigating exploit opportunities. Continuous monitoring and penetration testing, as well as revisiting security configurations, can help uncover any further vulnerabilities and bolster defenses across the board.
