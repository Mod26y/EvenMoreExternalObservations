Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/hackers-bypass-sonicwall-vpn-mfa-due-to-incomplete-patching/

1) What happened: Cybercriminals exploited a vulnerability in SonicWall Gen6 SSL-VPN appliances, specifically CVE-2024-12802, allowing them to bypass multi-factor authentication (MFA). They conducted network reconnaissance and tested credential reuse, ultimately deploying tools for potential ransomware attacks. Despite having updated firmware, systems remained vulnerable due to incomplete manual remediation of LDAP server configurations. This vulnerability primarily affects Gen6 devices, whereas Gen7 and Gen8 devices can be fully secured with a firmware update.

2) Why it matters: The incident highlights the importance of comprehensive patch management and following through on all remediation steps when addressing vulnerabilities. The ability of threat actors to bypass MFA—an essential security layer—demonstrates the evolving sophistication of cyber threats. This incident also serves as a cautionary tale about the risks associated with end-of-life devices, as Gen6 appliances have reached end-of-life status and no longer receive security updates, making them attractive targets for cybercriminals.

3) What actions should be taken: Organizations using SonicWall Gen6 SSL-VPN appliances should immediately implement the manual remediation actions outlined by SonicWall, such as reconfiguring LDAP settings, to close the vulnerability loop. Additionally, it is advisable to upgrade to newer Gen7 or Gen8 devices to ensure robust security support. Organizations should also enhance monitoring for suspicious VPN login activity, paying special attention to signs like the "CLI" session indicator and anomalous login patterns. Emphasizing regular cybersecurity training and readiness for swift incident response would further help in mitigating such vulnerabilities.
