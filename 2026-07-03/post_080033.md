Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/microsoft/microsoft-fixes-bug-that-removed-copilot-button-in-outlook/

Microsoft has resolved an issue that led to the disappearance of the Copilot Chat or Copilot buttons in the Classic Outlook for users with the Copilot Chat (Basic) license. This issue made the Copilot icon and buttons inaccessible, although the functionality remained available via other entry points. The Outlook Team implemented a service change on June 29, 2026, to address the problem, recommending a restart of the email client and updates to the latest build for any remaining issues. They are also looking into Outlook crashes linked to Kaspersky Antivirus.

This issue matters because it disrupts workflow and productivity for users relying on the Copilot feature for efficiency enhancements in Classic Outlook. Persistent bugs could lead to frustrations and reduced trust in Microsoft's services. Similarly, ongoing compatibility and crashing issues with prevalent antivirus software; such as Kaspersky, have the potential to pose risks to business operations and security postures.

In response to this information, users should ensure their Outlook clients are updated to the latest version and restart their application to make any unseen changes take effect. Monitoring Microsoft and Kaspersky support channels for further updates on compatibility issues is advisable. Organizations might consider performing regular testing and simulation exercises with their cybersecurity infrastructure to identify and mitigate system vulnerabilities proactively.
