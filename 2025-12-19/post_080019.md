Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/microsoft/windows-10-oob-update-released-to-fix-message-queuing-msmq-issues/

The article reports an issue following a December 2025 security update for Windows 10 that disrupted the functionality of Message Queuing (MSMQ), a tool primarily employed in enterprise environments to handle background operations. The malfunction might lead to inactive queues, resource errors, or issues with writing messages, especially in clustered setups. Consequently, Microsoft has issued an out-of-band update (KB5074976) through the Update Catalog, not accessible through standard update channels like WSUS.

The problem with MSMQ is significant because it can compromise an organization's ability to efficiently manage background tasks, impacting business operations that rely on queued messaging systems. Enterprise environments, which might deploy numerous applications depending on MSMQ, could face disruptions, reducing productivity and leading to potential losses. The highlighted issues underscore the importance of maintaining robust testing procedures in security updates to prevent operational hindrances in complex IT infrastructures.

In response, IT departments in affected enterprises should download and deploy the KB5074976 update from the Update Catalog to restore MSMQ functionality. It is crucial to test this update in a controlled environment before widespread deployment to ensure compatibility and stability. Additionally, organizations should review and update their patch management and testing strategies to better anticipate potential disruptions from future updates. Engaging with Microsoft support for guidance can also help in mitigating similar issues.
