Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/university-of-sydney-suffers-data-breach-exposing-student-and-staff-info/

1) What happened: Hackers accessed the University of Sydney's online coding repository, stealing personal data belonging to over 27,000 individuals, including staff and students. This breach involved personal information files that should not have been stored in a development environment primarily used for code storage. The unauthorized access was swiftly identified and blocked, and relevant authorities were notified.

2) Why it matters: This breach highlights vulnerabilities in data management and storage policies, particularly concerning the security of non-essential data in development environments. As one of Australia's prominent academic institutions, the incident can harm the university's reputation and trust. It could potentially expose sensitive personal information to misuse, which requires careful handling and response.

3) Actions to be taken: The university should conduct a comprehensive risk assessment and review of data management policies, emphasizing the principle of least privilege to prevent similar breaches. Training for staff on data protection and security practices, including reinforcing the segregation of sensitive data from development environments, should be instituted. Potentially affected individuals should be encouraged to monitor for identity theft and implement security measures like changing passwords and enabling MFA. Affected parties should also receive ongoing updates and support throughout the investigation process.
