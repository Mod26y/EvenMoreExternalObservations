**1) What happened?**

The UK National Cyber Security Centre (NCSC) has issued a warning to organizations about increased risks of Iranian cyberattacks amid Middle Eastern conflicts. While there is no immediate change in Iran's threat level to the UK, the situation could escalate suddenly, especially for entities with operations or supply chains in the Middle East. Despite widespread internet blackouts in Iran, state-affiliated hackers retain the capacity to launch attacks. Organizations should be vigilant and follow guidance on DDoS attacks, phishing, and industrial control system targeting to bolster security.

**2) Why it matters?**

This alert highlights the potential for geopolitical tensions to spill over into cyberspace, putting organizations at risk, especially those with assets and ties to conflict zones. It underscores that even during internet restrictions, state-backed actors can execute cyber attacks, which could result in compromised networks, data breaches, or disruptions in service. Remaining alert is essential not only in the UK but globally, illustrating the interconnected nature of modern supply chains and the potential broad impact of a regional conflict on global operations.

**3) What actions should be taken?**

Organizations, particularly those with operations in or ties to the Middle East, should proactively assess and potentially enhance their cybersecurity strategies based on NCSC recommendations. This includes reviewing and improving defenses against DDoS attacks and phishing, monitoring external threats more rigorously, and securing Industrial Control Systems. Keeping up-to-date with cybersecurity advisories from national agencies and ensuring that all staff are aware of potential threats can foster a culture of vigilance and preparedness. Engaging in scenario planning for cyber incident response could further strengthen resilience against potential attacks.
