Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/microsoft/microsoft-defender-rogueplanet-zero-day-grants-system-privileges/

The security researcher, known as Nightmare Eclipse, revealed a new zero-day vulnerability in Microsoft Defender called "RoguePlanet" shortly after Microsoft addressed previous flaws. This vulnerability exploits a race condition in Windows 10 and 11, potentially allowing attackers to open a command prompt with SYSTEM privileges. Despite Microsoft's protective measures, the researcher demonstrated through a self-hosted Git repository that their proof-of-concept could bypass existing defenses. The exploit's release is part of an ongoing conflict with Microsoft over vulnerability disclosure practices.

The incident is significant as it highlights the challenges in timely and effective vulnerability disclosure and the potential risks posed by unpatched zero-day exploits. Moreover, the release underscores vulnerabilities even in fully updated systems, emphasizing the critical need for proactive cybersecurity measures. The situation also draws attention to the tension between researchers and companies over disclosure practices, with the potential to harm users whose systems may become compromised due to delayed responses or unresolved tensions.

Organizations should consider implementing multiple layers of security, such as application allowlisting and robust monitoring, to mitigate the risk posed by vulnerabilities like RoguePlanet. Continuously updating all systems and employing breach and attack simulations can further protect against undisclosed or recently released exploits. Engaging with both vendors and the cybersecurity community in collaborative vulnerability management can also improve response times to emerging threats.
