Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/australia-warns-of-global-campaign-targeting-vulnerable-cms-platforms/

**1) What happened:** The Australian Cyber Security Centre (ACSC) has issued an alert regarding an active global campaign targeting vulnerabilities in Content Management Systems (CMS) and related plugins. Multiple Australian businesses have already been compromised, with threat actors deploying webshells to gain persistent access to affected sites. These webshells enable attackers to disrupt services, steal credentials, install additional malware, and infiltrate deeper into networks. The campaign exploits flaws in various CMS platforms, including WordPress, Craft CMS, MaxSite CMS, MetInfo CMS, and Joomla, with specific vulnerabilities identified in numerous WordPress plugins.

**2) Why it matters:** This campaign underscores a significant threat to small and medium-sized businesses relying on CMS platforms, which are often foundational to their digital operations. The use of webshells by cyber actors facilitates clandestine operations within affected systems, allowing for potential data theft, service disruption, and broader network infiltration. The potential involvement of AI accelerates attack processes, increasing the risk to businesses lacking robust cybersecurity measures. This threat is particularly concerning as CMS platforms are widely used, making the campaign's impact potentially far-reaching and challenging to mitigate.

**3) What actions should be taken:** Organizations should prioritize applying the latest security updates for their CMS platforms and associated plugins. Implementing measures such as removing unused components, enabling automatic updates, and making web directories read-only where feasible can enhance security. Regular monitoring for unauthorized file creation, restricting access to sensitive directories, and preventing unexpected process spawning are recommended to fortify defenses. Organizations should also consider adopting advanced security practices such as breach and attack simulations to test and improve their defenses, ensuring their systems are resilient against evolving cyber threats.
