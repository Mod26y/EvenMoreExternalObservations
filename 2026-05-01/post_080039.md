### What Happened

The KB5083769 security update released in April 2026 for Windows 11 versions 24H2 and 25H2 disrupts third-party backup software that utilizes Volume Shadow Copy Service (VSS), leading to failures in backup operations. This issue is largely due to a VSS service timeout affecting products from various vendors, including Acronis, Macrium, and UrBackup. Acronis confirmed that Windows 11 Pro and Home editions are particularly affected, resulting in backup errors and, occasionally, loss of connectivity with cloud services.

### Why It Matters

This update significantly impacts organizations relying on these backup solutions for disaster recovery and data integrity, critical components of robust cybersecurity strategy. Backup failures expose organizations to increased risks of data loss and complicate recovery processes following potential incidents such as ransomware attacks. Ensuring reliable backup systems is foundational for maintaining business continuity and protecting sensitive information, making it essential to address this issue swiftly to mitigate potential damages.

### Actions to Be Taken

Organizations should consider temporarily rolling back the problematic KB5083769 update on affected systems to restore backup functionality. Pausing further Windows updates until a fix or official patch from Microsoft becomes available is advisable to prevent recurrence. IT departments need to stay informed about Microsoft's response for a long-term solution and ensure compliance with any guidance issued. Additionally, affected organizations should reassess their backup strategies and vendor dependences, perhaps seeking backup solutions less reliant on VSS or with alternative mechanisms to handle potential disruptions.
