Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/european-commission-confirms-data-breach-after-europaeu-hack/

1) The European Commission experienced a data breach after their Europa.eu web platform was attacked by the ShinyHunters extortion gang. The breach affected at least one Amazon Web Services (AWS) account belonging to the Commission, resulting in the theft of over 350 GB of data. Despite the breach, Europa websites remained operational. The Commission is actively investigating the incident, notifying affected parties, and taking measures to bolster their internal security systems.

2) The incident highlights significant vulnerabilities in cloud-based infrastructure, even for influential bodies like the European Commission. With over 350 GB of sensitive information potentially exposed, the breach underscores the importance of robust cybersecurity measures across all platforms, particularly in government agencies handling large volumes of confidential data. The incident also raises concerns about the capabilities of organized cybercrime groups, such as ShinyHunters, to exploit security gaps for extortion.

3) In response to this information, organizations should consider conducting comprehensive security audits of their cloud services to identify and remediate vulnerabilities. Implementing multi-factor authentication and reinforcing access controls, especially for third-party services, would be prudent measures. Additionally, regular training programs for employees on recognizing phishing and social engineering attacks could help prevent unauthorized access. Finally, organizations might routinely update and test their incident response strategies to ensure swift action during a breach.
