Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/optinmonster-wordpress-plugin-hacked-in-cdn-supply-chain-attack/

**What happened:** The OptinMonster WordPress plugin, part of Awesome Motive's product suite, was compromised in a CDN supply-chain attack. Hackers exploited a vulnerability in the UpdraftPlus WordPress plugin, gaining access to Awesome Motive's server hosting credentials for their CDN account. They injected malicious JavaScript into the CDN, affecting OptinMonster, TrustPulse, and PushEngage users. The scripts collected administrative credentials from website administrators, creating rogue admin accounts and installing a self-hiding backdoor, facilitating full control of compromised sites.

**Why it matters:** This incident highlights the vulnerabilities in third-party components and the widespread impact of CDN supply-chain attacks on website security. With at least 1.2 million websites using OptinMonster, the potential reach and damage are significant, especially for the eCommerce sites that rely on these plugins for marketing and conversion optimization. Such attacks compromise sensitive user data and potentially result in loss of trust, reputation damage, and financial loss for affected businesses.

**What actions should be taken:** Site owners are advised to thoroughly check for rogue admin accounts and hidden backdoor plugins. Immediate steps include executing server-side malware scans, rotating administrator passwords, API keys, and other sensitive credentials. Updating all plugins and monitoring for unusual activity is also critical. Organizations should conduct comprehensive security audits and simulations to improve incident detection and resilience against future attacks. Additionally, ensuring that known vulnerabilities in any integrated plugins are patched promptly can mitigate potential risks.
