Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/rondodox-botnet-malware-now-hacks-servers-using-xwiki-flaw/

The RondoDox botnet malware exploits a critical RCE vulnerability in the XWiki Platform, tracked as CVE-2025-24893, allowing attackers to execute remote shell payloads on vulnerable servers. This vulnerability was recently marked by CISA as actively exploited. Threat actors, including botnet operators and cryptocurrency miners, use this flaw. By injecting specially crafted code through the XWiki SolrSearch endpoint, the malware can download and execute malicious scripts, further spreading its influence and exploiting compromised systems.

This development is crucial as it highlights the increasing sophistication and scale of botnet threats like RondoDox, emphasizing the need for organizations reliant on the XWiki Platform to prioritize patching to mitigate these threats. Given its open-source nature and widespread use in internal knowledge management systems, unpatched vulnerabilities carry significant risks for data breach and unauthorized access, exposing sensitive information and critical infrastructure to potential cyberattacks.

Organizations should promptly update the XWiki Platform to versions 15.10.11 or 16.4.1, which are safe from this vulnerability. They should also monitor for Indicators of Compromise (IoCs) related to RondoDox activities and implement network scanning and intrusion detection systems to alert on suspicious activities, especially those attempting to exploit this particular flaw. Enhanced vigilance on server activities and updating security policies in line with recent advisories from cybersecurity agencies like CISA are also recommended steps to strengthen defenses.
