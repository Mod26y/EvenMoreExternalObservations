Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/mandiant-reveals-how-cisco-sd-wan-zero-day-attacks-gained-root-access/

1) In March 2026, hackers exploited a zero-day vulnerability, CVE-2026-20245, in Cisco Catalyst SD-WAN components to gain root access on targeted devices by carrying out command injection through a crafted file, following unauthorized peering connections on service provider infrastructure. Mandiant revealed that attackers initially accessed networks using previous Cisco SD-WAN authentication bypass flaws and then escalated privileges using the CVE-2026-20245 vulnerability. Attackers employed anti-forensic methods to obscure their activities and maintain their presence without detection.

2) This incident highlights significant risks posed by vulnerabilities in critical infrastructure such as Cisco's SD-WAN, which is used to manage complex network configurations globally. The exploitation of such vulnerabilities allows attackers to gain and maintain unauthorized access, potentially affecting large volumes of sensitive data and critical services. It underscores the importance of promptly addressing disclosed vulnerabilities and the potential impact of delayed patching or insufficient security measures in network management systems.

3) Organizations should upgrade their systems to patched software versions supplied by Cisco to mitigate the vulnerability. It is crucial to regularly audit and monitor for unauthorized configuration changes and peering connections to detect early signs of breaches. They can use Mandiant's indicators of compromise to assess whether their devices have been affected. Additionally, enhancing the validation of user-supplied inputs and employing anti-forensic and breach detection measures can bolster defenses against similar threats. Regular security audits and incorporating breach and attack simulations will also enhance threat detection and response capabilities.
