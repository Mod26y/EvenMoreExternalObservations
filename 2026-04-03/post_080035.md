Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/drift-loses-280-million-north-korean-hackers-seize-security-council-powers/

**1) What happened:** Drift Protocol, a decentralized finance (DeFi) trading platform built on Solana, suffered a significant security breach orchestrated by North Korean hackers. The hackers seized control of the platform’s Security Council administrative powers and drained approximately $280 million in assets. The attack involved premeditated steps, including setting up durable nonce accounts and obtaining multisig approvals from Security Council members to launch pre-signed malicious transactions, which transferred admin control to the attackers. This breach caused a halt in protocol functions, affecting borrow/lend deposits, vault deposits, and trading funds.

**2) Why it matters:** This incident underscores the vulnerabilities in decentralized finance platforms and the sophisticated tactics used by state-sponsored threat actors like those from North Korea. Such attacks could undermine trust in DeFi platforms, affecting their adoption and encouraging more regulatory scrutiny. The breach also highlights the importance of secure multisig operations and real-time monitoring to prevent such exploits. The financial loss affects not only the platform but also users who rely on it for trading and asset management, potentially leading to broader market implications.

**3) What actions should be taken:** Drift Protocol should prioritize completing and publishing a thorough post-mortem analysis. Enhancing the security of multisig operations by implementing additional verification layers and real-time anomaly detection could mitigate similar threats in the future. Collaborating closely with blockchain intelligence firms and law enforcement can aid in recovering funds and tracing the attackers. The incident emphasizes the necessity for continuous security audits and user education on recognizing red flags. Other DeFi platforms might also consider revising their security frameworks and practices based on insights from this incident.
