Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/residential-proxies-evaded-ip-reputation-checks-in-78-percent-of-4b-sessions/

The article describes how residential proxies are increasingly used to evade IP reputation checks due to their dynamic and short-lived nature. Researchers, using a dataset of 4 billion sessions, found that 78% of these sessions could not be detected by reputation systems, highlighting a significant gap in current network defense strategies. Residential proxies, frequently rotated and used only once or twice, originate from both IoT botnets and compromised devices. The analysis shows that these proxies were involved in activities such as network scanning, recon, and targeting enterprise VPNs, making them a challenge for conventional defenses.

This issue is crucial because it undermines the reliability of IP reputation systems, commonly used in network defense strategies. The stealth capabilities of residential proxies pose a security risk to organizations by allowing attackers to bypass detection and engage in unauthorized network activities. Moreover, the global reach and diversity of proxies, combined with their ability to mask malicious behavior, stress the need for evolving security methodologies beyond traditional IP-based checks. This development compels organizations to rethink their security frameworks to address these elusive threats effectively.

Organizations are recommended to shift focus from solely using IP reputation checks to adopting behavior-based detection methods. This includes identifying sequential probing patterns and monitoring for tasks that exploit weaknesses around device fingerprint permanence, despite IP changes. Blocking non-standard protocols from ISPs and incorporating advanced threat intelligence that aligns with the latest tactics used by proxy networks will enhance defenses. Engaging with network behavior analytics can better distinguish between legitimate and malicious activities, and continuous security training should be promoted alongside investing in robust detection technologies.
