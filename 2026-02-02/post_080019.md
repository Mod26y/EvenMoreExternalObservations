Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/apple/new-apple-privacy-feature-limits-location-tracking-on-iphones-ipads/

1) What happened: Apple has rolled out a new privacy feature that allows users to limit the precision of location data shared with cellular networks on certain iPhone and iPad models. This feature, called "Limit Precise Location," restricts cellular networks to approximate location data, such as a neighborhood rather than a precise address. Available for devices running iOS 26.3 or later, this feature does not affect emergency services or location data shared with apps via Location Services, and is currently supported by a few mobile networks globally.

2) Why it matters: The introduction of this privacy feature is significant in the context of ongoing concerns and regulatory actions related to location tracking and data privacy. For instance, the largest U.S. wireless carriers were fined nearly $200 million by the FCC for improper handling of location data. By offering this feature, Apple empowers users with more control over their data, potentially mitigating risks associated with unauthorized tracking and misuse of personal information, thus aligning with growing demands for digital privacy and compliance.

3) What actions should be taken: Information security teams within organizations, particularly those using iOS devices, should evaluate the implications of this newly released feature on their existing privacy policies and data management strategies. They should consider developing employee awareness programs to ensure users are aware of how to enable and use the "Limit Precise Location" setting. Additionally, they should engage with mobile network providers to better understand support and implementation timelines, and assess any broader organizational impacts linked to adopting this feature.
