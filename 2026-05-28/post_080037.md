**What happened?**  
The FBI has issued a warning that the Silent Ransom Group (SRG), an extortion gang, is conducting in-person data theft attacks on U.S. law firms. The attackers employ social engineering tactics, posing as IT employees to gain access through phone and phishing emails. If those attempts fail, the group sends a member in person to connect an external storage device to the victim's computer. The stolen data is then used for extortion, with threats to expose or sell the information unless a ransom is paid.

**Why it matters?**  
The SRG's activities pose a significant threat to sensitive data within law firms and could lead to financial loss, legal liabilities, and reputational damage. The use of both remote and in-person techniques signals a versatile approach that can be harder to defend against. This trend highlights the evolving nature of cybersecurity threats where traditional security awareness training must be coupled with robust physical security measures to protect sensitive data in legal and financial sectors.

**What actions should be taken?**  
Organizations should enhance their security training programs to include education on recognizing social engineering attacks, especially those involving IT department impersonation. It is also important to establish strict verification procedures for granting remote or physical access to IT systems. Additionally, implementing technical controls, such as monitoring for unauthorized use of USB devices, and ensuring physical security measures are in place to prevent unauthorized access to company premises, could help mitigate such threats. Cybersecurity teams should regularly collaborate with IT and physical security departments to assess vulnerabilities and update protocols accordingly.
