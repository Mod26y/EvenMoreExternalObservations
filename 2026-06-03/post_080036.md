Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/critical-kirki-flaw-exploited-to-hijack-wordpress-admin-accounts/

Hackers have exploited a critical privilege escalation flaw in the Kirki plugin for WordPress, identified as CVE-2026-8206. This flaw allows attackers to hijack user accounts by sending password reset links to an arbitrary email address they control. The compromise occurs due to a vulnerability in the 'handle_forgot_password()' function of the plugin’s REST API. Over 222 exploit attempts were observed in 24 hours, affecting more than 500,000 websites using the plugin, with major exploitation affecting versions 6.0.0 to 6.0.6 of the plugin.

This incident underscores the inherent risks of third-party plugins, particularly in widely-used content management systems like WordPress. Given that the flaw can easily grant attackers administrative access, it poses a severe security risk, potentially allowing unauthorized control over websites, data breaches, or further malicious activity. Due to the broad reach of this plugin, the vulnerability’s exploitation could have widespread implications for website integrity and data protection.

Website administrators should immediately update to version 6.0.7 of the Kirki plugin, which solves the vulnerability. Alternatively, temporarily disabling the plugin could mitigate immediate threats until an upgrade is feasible. It's critical to regularly audit and monitor the use of third-party plugins, ensuring they are up-to-date and not vulnerable to exploitation. Effective incident response plans and backups can further help manage the fallout from such vulnerabilities.
