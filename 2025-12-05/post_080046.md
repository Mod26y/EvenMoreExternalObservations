Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/cisa-warns-of-chinese-brickstorm-malware-attacks-on-vmware-servers/

The article reports that the U.S. Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA) has identified and warned against a cyberattack campaign by Chinese hackers using the Brickstorm malware to backdoor VMware vSphere servers. This malware creates rogue virtual machines for data exfiltration and credential theft, exhibiting sophisticated evasion tactics like encryption and self-monitoring. These attacks, linked to the Chinese group Warp Panda, have targeted sectors like legal, technology, and manufacturing from April 2024 to September 2025. Hackers exploited vulnerabilities to access critical systems and maintain persistence over extended periods.

This development matters because it highlights ongoing and sophisticated cyber threats targeting critical infrastructure and sensitive sectors in the U.S. The use of VMware environments as a vector for these attacks underscores the need for increased vigilance and security measures around widely deployed virtualization technologies. It also points to the geopolitical dynamics of cyber warfare, with state-sponsored actors continually evolving their attack methods to penetrate even robust security postures.

Organizations should proactively implement CISA's recommended actions to detect and mitigate these threats. This includes using YARA and Sigma rules to identify indicators of compromise, blocking unauthorized DNS-over-HTTPS traffic, and segmenting networks to limit unauthorized lateral movement. Moreover, organizations should inventory and monitor network devices and report any attack indicators to authorities. Continuous updating of security protocols and frameworks to address emerging threats would further bolster defenses against such persistent, state-sponsored cyber-attacks.
