**What happened:** The article likely discusses how incorporating robust software development practices can help organizations address tangible cybersecurity risks. This may include methodologies like secure coding, continuous integration/continuous deployment (CI/CD), and DevSecOps that prioritize security throughout the software development lifecycle. These practices aim to mitigate vulnerabilities before products are deployed, reducing the risk of exploitation by cyber threats.

**Why it matters:** In today’s digital landscape, software vulnerabilities can be entry points for cyberattacks. Implementing security-focused development practices can significantly reduce potential attack surfaces. This proactive approach is crucial as organizations face increasingly sophisticated threats that can lead to data breaches, financial loss, and reputational damage. Incorporating security early in the development process enables enterprises to address potential vulnerabilities and aligns with cybersecurity resilience goals.

**What actions should be taken:** Organizations would benefit from evaluating and enhancing their software development processes by integrating security at every stage. Adopting secure coding guidelines, conducting regular security assessments, and training developers in best practices are vital steps. Additionally, fostering a culture that prioritizes cybersecurity within development teams encourages vigilance against emerging threats. Regularly updating and patching software can further enhance security posture as threats evolve.
