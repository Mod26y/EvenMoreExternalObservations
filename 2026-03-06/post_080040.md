Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/wikipedia-hit-by-self-propagating-javascript-worm-that-vandalized-pages/

A self-propagating JavaScript worm infected Wikimedia's Meta-Wiki by modifying user scripts, resulting in vandalized pages. It started with a malicious script executed on a Wikimedia employee account, spreading across the platform by injecting loaders into user-specific and global scripts. Approximately 3,996 pages and 85 user scripts were altered before engineers restricted editing, disabled the scripts, and reverted changes.

This incident highlights the vulnerabilities within wiki platforms where user-generated scripts can be manipulated to launch widespread attacks. The speed of its spread and the impact on platform integrity emphasize the need for robust security measures around user-authored code, underscoring its potential risk.

In response, organizations managing wiki platforms should enhance scrutiny of user-generated scripts and employ rigorous auditing and validation of user scripts. Conducting regular security reviews and establishing incident response procedures can aid in swift detection and mitigation of similar threats. Additionally, investing in security training for staff can prevent accidental execution of malicious code.
