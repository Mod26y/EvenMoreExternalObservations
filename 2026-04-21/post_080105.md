Original Article: https://www.darkreading.com/threat-intelligence/nist-cutbacks-nvd-handling-impacts-cyber-teams

1) The National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) has reduced its involvement in handling the Common Vulnerabilities and Exposures (CVE) through its National Vulnerability Database (NVD). This cutback means that the agency will process fewer vulnerabilities, impacting the speed and quantity of public vulnerability disclosures. Cybersecurity teams rely heavily on these disclosures to identify and mitigate threats.

2) This reduction is significant because NIST’s NVD is a key resource for cybersecurity practitioners globally. With fewer processed vulnerabilities, there may be delays in identifying emerging threats, leaving systems potentially exposed to exploits. For cyber teams, this means an increased workload to manually verify and track emerging vulnerabilities.

3) Cyber teams should consider supplementing the NVD with other vulnerability intelligence sources. Building internal processes to triage vulnerabilities and using automation tools can enhance their ability to respond swiftly. Additionally, increased collaboration with other organizations and industry sharing platforms may help in bridging any gaps caused by NIST's reduced CVE handling. Investing in training to improve analysis and increase awareness of vulnerability trends could also be beneficial.
