Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/microsoft-teams-increasingly-abused-in-helpdesk-impersonation-attacks/

1) What happened: Microsoft has reported a rise in cyberattacks where threat actors exploit Microsoft Teams to impersonate IT or helpdesk staff. They engage with employees via cross-tenant chats, tricking them into providing remote machine access under the guise of resolving technical issues. This access is then used to perform data theft, leveraging legitimate software and tools such as Quick Assist and Rclone for file transfers, thus blending their activities into normal network operations.

2) Why it matters: These attacks showcase a sophisticated method of social engineering and abuse of trusted communication channels and tools. They highlight the ongoing challenge of discerning malicious activity that masquerades as legitimate IT operations within organizations. This further stresses the need for heightened awareness and caution in handling unexpected external communications, as well as scrutiny of remote access requests, to safeguard sensitive data and maintain organizational security.

3) What actions should be taken: Organizations should treat external Teams contacts with skepticism, closely monitoring and potentially restricting the use of remote assistance tools. Limiting the use of Windows Remote Management (WinRM) to essential systems can also mitigate risk. Furthermore, training employees to recognize phishing attempts and reinforcing security protocols around communications will enhance organizational defenses. Implementing monitoring mechanisms for unusual network behaviors and verifying the authenticity of IT support communications can further bolster security.
