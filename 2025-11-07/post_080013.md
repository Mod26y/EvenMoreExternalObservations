The U.S. Congressional Budget Office (CBO) experienced a cybersecurity breach attributed to a suspected foreign attacker, potentially exposing sensitive information such as emails and exchanges with congressional offices, as well as draft reports and forecasts. The CBO reacted promptly to the threat by containing the breach, enhancing security measures, and continuing their current work with Congress. This incident is part of a broader trend of cyberattacks targeting U.S. government agencies, specifically by the Chinese state-sponsored group Silk Typhoon, which has exploited vulnerabilities like the ProxyLogon flaw in Microsoft Exchange Server in the past.

The breach holds significant implications as the CBO provides crucial economic analysis and legislative cost estimates that influence U.S. policy-making. Exposure of draft reports and communications could lead to information manipulation, policy disruption, and a loss of trust in governmental processes. Furthermore, this incident underscores the persistent threat from foreign entities targeting U.S. governmental bodies, highlighting vulnerabilities that could compromise national security and affect economic decisions and legislative processes.
