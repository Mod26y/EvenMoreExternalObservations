Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/eurail-says-stolen-traveler-data-now-up-for-sale-on-dark-web/

Eurail B.V., managing European rail pass sales, confirmed that customer data stolen in a prior breach is now for sale on the dark web. The breach exposed sensitive information including names, passport details, bank account IBANs, and more. A sample of this data was also shared on Telegram. The company is working to ascertain the specific records affected and notified relevant data protection authorities in compliance with GDPR.

This incident matters due to the potential consequences for affected customers, such as identity theft, financial fraud, and phishing attempts. It highlights the vulnerabilities within the travel sector dealing with extensive personal data. Given the popularity of Eurail passes, especially among young travelers in programs like DiscoverEU, a large number of individuals may be impacted, necessitating proactive measures for protecting personal information.

As an immediate response, customers should change passwords and closely monitor financial accounts for unusual activity, reporting concerns to their banks. Organizations, including Eurail, should enhance cybersecurity measures, conduct regular security audits, and provide customers with comprehensive information and support. Implementing multifactor authentication and strengthening access controls could help prevent future breaches. Consider reviewing and updating incident response protocols and ensuring employees are educated on emerging cybersecurity threats.
