Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/infostealer-malware-found-stealing-openclaw-secrets-for-first-time/

The incident involves the discovery of information-stealing malware targeting OpenClaw, a widely adopted AI assistant platform formerly known as ClawdBot and MoltBot. This malware was found stealing sensitive data such as API keys and authentication tokens from configuration files. The malware, a variant of the Vidar infostealer, does not specifically target OpenClaw but indiscriminately searches for files with keywords like "token" and "private key," compromising the digital identities of users by accessing critical data stored within OpenClaw's framework.

This event highlights a pivotal shift in cyber threats, with infostealers evolving from targeting browser credentials to extracting sensitive data from AI systems integrated into business and personal workflows. OpenClaw's widespread use and relatively weak security have made it a lucrative target. The ability to access authentication tokens and private keys can lead to full digital identity compromise, posing a severe risk to personal and organizational data privacy and security. As AI tools increasingly interlace with daily operations, understanding and mitigating these vulnerabilities becomes critical.

Organizations should evaluate and enhance security protocols focusing on AI frameworks like OpenClaw. This includes securing configuration files, encrypting sensitive data, and improving monitoring for unauthorized access. Implementing robust incident response strategies and employee training on recognizing potential phishing and infostealer deployment tactics can also mitigate these risks. Additionally, organizations should stay informed about vulnerabilities in related AI tools, such as the noted flaw in Nanobot, and apply patches promptly to prevent exploitations.
