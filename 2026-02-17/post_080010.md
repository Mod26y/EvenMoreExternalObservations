Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/washington-hotel-in-japan-discloses-ransomware-infection-incident/

1) What happened:
Washington Hotel in Japan faced a ransomware attack that compromised its servers, exposing various business data. The attack occurred on February 13, 2026, prompting the hotel's IT staff to disconnect from the internet to halt the spread. An internal task force and external cybersecurity experts were engaged to assess the damage, mitigate the attack's effects, and verify the safety of customer data, which appears unaffected due to its storage on separate servers. The attack caused temporary issues with credit card terminals but no major disruptions.

2) Why it matters:
This incident underscores the vulnerability of hospitality industry networks to cyber threats, highlighting the potential risks to business operations and customer trust. With Washington Hotel operating 30 locations in Japan, serving nearly 5 million guests yearly, any data compromise could lead to significant reputational and financial impact. The rising number of cyberattacks in Japan, including those on major corporations, indicates an urgent need for robust cybersecurity measures to protect sensitive data and ensure the continuity of critical operations.

3) What actions should be taken:
The Washington Hotel can collaborate with cybersecurity experts to conduct a thorough post-incident analysis to understand the breach specifics and enhance systems against future threats. Ongoing communication with stakeholders, including transparency about findings and remedial actions, will be crucial in managing reputational risk. Considering the broader trend of attacks, the hospitality sector might explore industry-wide collaborations to share threat intelligence and best practices. Regular security audits, employee training, and updates to technology infrastructure should form key parts of long-term cybersecurity strategies to prevent similar incidents.
