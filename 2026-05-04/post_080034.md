Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/critrical-cpanel-flaw-mass-exploited-in-sorry-ransomware-attacks/

1) What happened: A critical vulnerability in cPanel, identified as CVE-2026-41940, was discovered and is being actively exploited to conduct "Sorry" ransomware attacks. Hackers exploit this authentication bypass flaw to gain unauthorized access to web hosting control panels, breach websites, and encrypt data using a Go-based Linux encryptor. The flaw was being exploited since late February, and emergency updates have been released to address the vulnerability.

2) Why it matters: The exploitation of this cPanel vulnerability is significant due to the widespread impact it has on web hosting services and the potential for data loss or ransom demands. With over 44,000 IP addresses compromised, businesses and organizations using cPanel and WHM are at risk of service disruptions, financial losses, and reputational damage. The encryption mechanism of the ransomware makes file decryption impossible without the private RSA-2048 key, emphasizing the severity of failing to secure systems promptly.

3) What actions should be taken: Organizations utilizing cPanel and WHM should immediately prioritize installing the latest security updates to mitigate the risk of exploitation from the vulnerability. It is also advisable to enhance monitoring of their systems for unusual activities indicative of compromise. Administrators should review backup processes to ensure data is recoverable in case of an attack. Additionally, implementing advanced threat detection and response practices can help detect and halt such threats efficiently. Keeping security tools updated and training staff on phishing and ransomware risks can further strengthen defenses.
