Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/new-droidlock-malware-locks-android-devices-and-demands-a-ransom/

**1) What happened:**  
A new malware targeting Android devices, named DroidLock, has been identified. It predominantly targets Spanish-speaking users by being distributed through malicious websites that disguise the malware as legitimate applications. Once installed, DroidLock can lock a device's screen, access sensitive information, and carry out various harmful actions. It operates by using a droplet that deceives users, and then asks for Device Admin and Accessibility Services permissions to perform its malicious activities. The malware further exploits VNC sharing to control devices and employs overlays to steal lock patterns. Despite not encrypting data, it uses a ransomware overlay to threaten victims with the loss of their data unless a ransom is paid.

**2) Why it matters:**  
DroidLock is a significant threat as it represents the evolving tactics of malware developers who are targeting Android devices with increasing sophistication. This malware's capability to take full control of a device, commit data theft, and use psychological tactics by threatening data loss illustrates the critical risks it poses to personal and organizational data integrity. It underscores the vulnerabilities in current mobile security and the need for enhanced protective measures. This malicious software also reflects the need for increased vigilance, especially against the threat of social engineering attacks that trick users into downloading harmful applications.

**3) What actions should be taken:**  
To counter DroidLock and similar threats, Android users should avoid downloading applications from unofficial sources and be cautious of granting administrative permissions without verifying the legitimacy of the app. Regularly updating devices and employing security solutions like Google Play Protect can help detect and block such threats. Organizations should educate users about the dangers of sideloading apps and emphasize the importance of accessing applications from trusted sources. Moreover, a periodic review of the device settings and permissions granted to different applications would add an additional layer of security to prevent unauthorized access and control.
