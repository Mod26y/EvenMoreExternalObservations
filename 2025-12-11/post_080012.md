Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/google-ads-for-shared-chatgpt-grok-guides-push-macos-infostealer-malware/

1) **What happened:** An AMOS infostealer campaign targeting macOS users exploits Google search ads by redirecting users to tampered ChatGPT and Grok conversations. These conversations appear to offer guidance on common troubleshooting issues but lead to installing malware. When users follow the provided commands, they inadvertently download and execute a bash script, enabling the AMOS infostealer to access privileged system areas. The malware exploits vulnerable macOS systems by planting trojanized crypto-wallet apps and stealing sensitive data like passwords and cryptocurrency credentials, thus compromising user security.

2) **Why it matters:** This campaign illustrates evolving threat vectors exploiting popular and trusted platforms such as ChatGPT and Google Search ads. By manipulating legitimate channels and presenting convincingly genuine content, these malicious actors effectively deceive users into compromising their own security. The situation reveals how cybercriminals can manipulate AI-based platforms to deliver malware, highlighting the need for robust cybersecurity awareness and the risks of executing commands from unverified sources, which could lead to significant financial and privacy consequences for users.

3) **What actions should be taken:** Organizations and users should increase vigilance regarding online troubleshooting instructions, particularly those found on forums like ChatGPT. It's vital to implement comprehensive security awareness training, teaching users to verify the safety of instructions before executing them. Consider using ad blockers to minimize exposure to malicious ads and employing endpoint protection solutions that can detect and block unauthorized scripts. Additionally, organizations could enhance their incident response protocols to swiftly address potential malware compromises, and they should consider regular audits of public-facing interactions to identify potential abuse points.
