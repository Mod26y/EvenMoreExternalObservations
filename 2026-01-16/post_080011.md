Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/technology/google-now-lets-you-change-your-gmailcom-address-rolling-out/

1) What happened: Google has introduced a new feature that allows users to change their primary @gmail.com email address while maintaining access to their existing account and data. The old email becomes an alias, permitting users to send and receive emails from both addresses. Although the feature is rolling out gradually, it provides a significant change from Google's previous policy, where primary email addresses could not be altered.

2) Why it matters: This update enhances user flexibility and privacy, enabling individuals to adopt addresses that better represent them without losing access to their data or requiring a cumbersome account migration. It is particularly beneficial for those who may have chosen or been assigned embarrassing or outdated email addresses earlier. From a cybersecurity perspective, it underscores the need to educate users on managing alias addresses and understanding the security implications of changing primary identifiers.

3) What actions should be taken: Organizations should update their IT and cybersecurity policies to reflect changes in the management of Google accounts within their environments. Training should be provided to employees on the implications of using alias email addresses, ensuring they understand authentication practices and data continuity during email address changes. Additionally, IT teams should monitor for potential phishing attempts exploiting this new feature, as cyber attackers may attempt to deceive users by impersonating familiar email addresses with subtle alterations.
