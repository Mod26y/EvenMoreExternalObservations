Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/hackers-exploit-gladinet-centrestack-cryptographic-flaw-in-rce-attacks/

Hackers have exploited a cryptographic flaw in Gladinet's CentreStack and Triofox products enabling remote code execution (RCE). The vulnerability arises from a custom AES cryptographic algorithm implementation with hardcoded keys, allowing attackers to decrypt access tickets and impersonate users. The products’ cryptographic flaw doesn’t have an official identifier, but the vendor has released updates and indicators of compromise (IoCs). Researchers identified at least nine organizational victims across various sectors. The widespread nature and potential impact on sensitive data highlight the urgency of addressing the vulnerability.

This matters because it exposes organizations using these products to significant security risks, including unauthorized data access and potential system control by attackers. The flaw’s usage across multiple installations amplifies the risk, as attackers can readily execute unauthorized operations. Moreover, targeting sectors like healthcare and technology, which often handle highly sensitive data, underscores the potential for severe data breaches, privacy violations, and operational disruptions.

Organizations using Gladinet products should consider upgrading to the latest versions to mitigate the vulnerability promptly. They might also assess previous access logs for indicators of compromise, specifically the encrypted file path string. Rotating machine keys and employing Huntress’ mitigation guides can help secure their environments. Additionally, reinforcing staff awareness about security updates and potential threats can bolster their overall cybersecurity posture. Conducting a thorough security review to evaluate potential exposure could be beneficial in preventing future exploits.
