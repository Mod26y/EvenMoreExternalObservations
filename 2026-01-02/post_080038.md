Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/nyc-mayoral-inauguration-bans-flipper-zero-raspberry-pi-devices/

1) What happened: During the 2026 New York City mayoral inauguration of Zohran Mamdani, a list of prohibited items was issued that specifically bans the Flipper Zero and Raspberry Pi devices. These devices are commonly used for testing and learning wireless communication protocols and as low-cost compute platforms, respectively. The prohibition singles them out even though similar devices, such as laptops and mobile phones, which also have the capacity for advanced testing and penetration activities, are not banned. The reasoning behind the specific mention of these devices has not been explained by the event organizers.

2) Why it matters: The specific ban on Flipper Zero and Raspberry Pi devices highlights a broader concern over the misuse of everyday technology for malicious purposes such as cybercrime and unauthorized access to networks. Although these tools are legitimate for educational and developmental purposes, their capabilities also pose potential security threats at large public events. This situation raises awareness of the delicate balance between technology use for innovation and security concerns. Moreover, it exposes potential gaps in public understanding and policymaking regarding cybersecurity devices and how they are categorized.

3) What actions should be taken: The cybersecurity community, alongside policymakers, could use this event as an opportunity to engage in dialogue about the responsible use of cybersecurity tools while promoting better public understanding of such devices. Event organizers should consider consulting with cybersecurity professionals to ensure comprehensive security policies that mitigate risks without blanket prohibitions that may reflect a lack of understanding. Additionally, community outreach and education programs could help both the public and policy officials understand the capabilities and risks associated with these devices, leading to informed and balanced security measures.
