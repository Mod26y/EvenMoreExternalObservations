Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/microsoft/microsoft-accelerates-quantum-safe-roadmap-as-risks-grow/

**1) What happened:** Microsoft has announced an accelerated plan to integrate post-quantum cryptography (PQC) due to advancements in quantum computing. They aim to transition critical products and services to quantum-safe standards by 2029 as part of the Microsoft Quantum Safe Program. This shift seeks to protect against potential "harvest now, decrypt later" scenarios, where encrypted data is stolen and stored until it can be decrypted by future quantum computers. Microsoft's approach includes upgrading cryptographic networks, ensuring crypto-agility, and modernizing cryptographic trust chains to ease future transitions.

**2) Why it matters:** The rapid advancement in quantum computing presents a looming threat to traditional encryption methods. Quantum computers could potentially decrypt current encryption, making sensitive data vulnerable. This shift towards PQC is essential for organizations to protect data integrity against futuristic quantum threats. The announcement reflects a growing urgency in the industry to prioritize cybersecurity measures that anticipate future technologies. Organizations that delay integrating quantum-safe measures risk increased exposure to data breaches in the near future.

**3) What actions should be taken:** Organizations should begin implementing post-quantum cryptography by modernizing their current infrastructure to support future transitions. They should adopt updated network protocols like TLS 1.3 and focus on achieving crypto-agility, enabling a seamless switch to quantum-resistant algorithms when necessary. Additionally, entities should assess and modernize their cryptographic trust chains, such as those related to code signing and certificate management. It would also be beneficial to integrate these efforts into broader security readiness initiatives, ensuring coherence and adaptability as quantum computing technology evolves.
