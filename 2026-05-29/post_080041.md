Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/fbi-warns-of-fake-fifa-websites-running-world-cup-fraud-schemes/

**1) What happened?**

The FBI has issued a warning about fraudulent websites mimicking FIFA's official site ahead of the 2026 World Cup. These phishing sites deploy subtle tweaks in spelling and domain extensions, aiming to deceive visitors into sharing personal and financial data. The fraudulent operations sell fake tickets and merchandise related to the event, backed by malvertising campaigns found on platforms like Google, Facebook, Telegram, and WhatsApp. In particular, a significant portion of these phishing sites has been attributed to a Chinese threat actor group called Ghost Stadium.

**2) Why it matters?**

This cyber threat holds significant implications as it targets a large, diverse global audience due to the high-profile nature of the World Cup. The breaches of personal data not only lead to financial loss for individuals but also pose risks of identity theft and creation of fraudulent accounts. The scale and sophistication of these phishing campaigns demonstrate the capability of organized cybercriminal groups to exploit widely recognized global events. It highlights the necessity for increased vigilance and proactive measures in cybersecurity, especially for individuals engaging with content related to major events.

**3) What actions should be taken as a result of this information?**

To mitigate these threats, individuals should adopt recommended precautionary measures, such as manually typing official URLs, avoiding clicking on sponsored ads, and verifying website authenticity before entering sensitive information. Organizations, particularly those linked to such international events, should enhance their cybersecurity measures by informing potential targets about phishing risks and monitoring for fake websites. Reporting any fraudulent activity to authorities like the FBI's Internet Crime Complaint Center can aid in tracking and combating these schemes. Additionally, there should be a concerted effort to educate the public on identifying warning signs of fake websites and scams.
