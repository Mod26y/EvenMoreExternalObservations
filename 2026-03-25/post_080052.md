1) **What happened:** Mozilla has introduced a built-in VPN feature in Firefox 149, offering up to 50GB of monthly traffic specifically for browser use. This VPN routes traffic through a secure proxy, allowing users to hide their location and IP address while browsing. It allows selective site activation up to five websites to manage traffic usage, and certain sites bypass the VPN to ensure functionality. Data collection is limited to necessary technical information to monitor the VPN's performance. Initially, this feature is being rolled out to users in the U.S., UK, Germany, and France with further expansion plans unstated.

2) **Why it matters:** This development highlights an increasing focus on enhancing user privacy in web browsing, essential in an era where cyber threats and data breaches are rampant. By providing a free VPN option, Mozilla empowers users with a straightforward tool to safeguard their online activities, particularly in public and unsecured networks. The significance is also evident in the competitive browser market, where privacy features can influence user preference. The addition of security updates and fixes underscores Mozilla's dedication to ensuring both privacy and security, crucial for internet users' trust and safety.

3) **What actions should be taken:** The information security steering committee should consider recommending Firefox as a tool within the county's internet browsers due to its enhanced privacy features for users. Educating employees about the new built-in VPN functionality can enhance their data protection practices, especially when accessing sensitive information. Additionally, staying informed about updates and leveraging browsers that frequently address security vulnerabilities protects against emerging threats. Continuous monitoring of the performance and feedback of these tools will help in assessing their effectiveness and integration into broader cybersecurity strategies.
