Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/popular-litellm-pypi-package-compromised-in-teampcp-supply-chain-attack/

The incident involves the compromise of the popular LiteLLM Python package on PyPI by the hacking group TeamPCP, deploying an infostealer that exfiltrates sensitive information from infected systems. Two malicious versions of LiteLLM were released, which the attackers reportedly used to harvest data from approximately 500,000 instances, targeting credentials, cloud tokens, and other sensitive files across diverse networks. Given the massive download rate of LiteLLM, this attack presents significant risk, particularly for organizations employing the package in production environments.

This attack underscores the vulnerability of open-source software supply chains, as well as the potential for rapid and extensive compromise through popular packages like LiteLLM. Organizations relying on such packages are at heightened risk of data breaches, potentially resulting in unauthorized access, lateral movement within networks, and loss of sensitive data. The incident demonstrates the importance of securing the software supply chain and maintaining vigilance over third-party software components.

Organizations affected by the compromised LiteLLM versions should immediately conduct thorough assessments of their systems. It is advisable to rotate exposed credentials, including SSH keys and cloud authentication tokens, and inspect for artifacts indicative of persistent threats. Monitoring for suspicious activity, such as unauthorized pods in Kubernetes clusters and unexpected outbound traffic to known attacker domains, is recommended. These measures aim to mitigate unauthorized access and prevent further damage while ensuring the security of their systems and data integrity.
