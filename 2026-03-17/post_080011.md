Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/stryker-attack-wiped-tens-of-thousands-of-devices-no-malware-needed/

**1) What happened:**

Last week, Stryker, a medical technology company, experienced a cyberattack targeting its internal Microsoft environment, notably bypassing traditional malware use. Hackers exploited the Intune management service to remotely wipe data from around 80,000 devices, utilizing a compromised administrator account. The attacker is linked to the Handala hacktivist group, reportedly connected to Iran. No evidence of data exfiltration was found. The attack did not impact Stryker's medical products but affected its ordering and transactional systems.

**2) Why it matters:**

This incident highlights significant vulnerabilities in internal IT systems, demonstrating how attackers can exploit administrative privileges without deploying malware. The attack underscores the criticality of robust access controls and monitoring systems for account management. The repercussions on Stryker’s operations, including manual order processing and potential supply chain disruptions, serve as a stark reminder of operational risks connected to cybersecurity incidents. Stakeholders, especially in the healthcare sector, need to recognize the potentially far-reaching impacts of such attacks, even when core products remain untouched.

**3) What actions should be taken as a result of this information:**

Organizations should immediately review and enhance their access management protocols, ensuring restricted access to critical administrative accounts. Implementing multi-factor authentication and rigorous monitoring of account activities, especially in cloud environments, is vital. Companies should regularly audit their security policies and conduct employee awareness training on cybersecurity best practices. Incident response plans must be updated to swiftly address similar disruptions. Collaborating with cybersecurity experts can provide insights into fortifying defenses and detecting latent vulnerabilities. Additionally, reinforcing communication channels during incidents ensures continued operations and customer trust.
