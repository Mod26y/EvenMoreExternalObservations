Original Article: https://www.darkreading.com/threat-intelligence/attackers-livechat-phish-credit-card-personal-data

1) A phishing campaign has been discovered where attackers are leveraging live chat support systems on compromised websites to harvest credit card information and personal data. By integrating fraudulent chat windows, attackers can deceive users into sharing sensitive information. This novel tactic bypasses traditional phishing indicators, as users may perceive live chat widgets as legitimate customer support features.

2) This attack is significant as it illustrates an innovative exploitation of user trust in embedded web functionalities like live chat. Such methods pose a substantial risk because they can effectively bypass users’ previous phishing defenses, highlighting the evolving sophistication of cyber threats and the need for improved digital literacy and awareness.

3) Organizations should review and monitor the integration of third-party widgets and live chat functionalities on their websites. It would be prudent to educate users on recognizing unusual chat interactions and encourage the verification of support agent identities before sharing sensitive information. Regular security audits and the implementation of intrusion detection systems could help identify and mitigate such threats before they can impact users.
