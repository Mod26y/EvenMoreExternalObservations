Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/phishing-poses-as-big-brand-job-interview-to-steal-google-accounts/

1) What happened: A sophisticated phishing campaign has been targeting marketing professionals by impersonating job offers from over 30 high-profile brands, such as Adobe and Coca-Cola, to steal Google account credentials. The attackers exploit trusted platforms like the PeopleForce HR platform and Salesforce Marketing Cloud to direct victims to a fake landing page that mimics Google’s login portal. By utilizing real recruiter names and images, as well as leveraging nested redirects, the phishing campaign deceives recipients into entering their credentials, with the appearance of legitimacy enhanced by employing browser-in-the-browser attack techniques.

2) Why it matters: This campaign highlights the evolving sophistication in phishing tactics, making it harder for individuals to distinguish between genuine and malicious interactions. By targeting marketing professionals, who often have access to sensitive company information and accounts, the attack poses significant risks of information breaches and financial losses for companies. The exploitation of legitimate platforms and the realistic presentation of phishing materials further underscore the critical need for advanced cybersecurity awareness and protective measures in enterprises and among professionals.

3) What actions should be taken: It's crucial to strengthen email and web security systems to detect and block deceptive phishing campaigns. Companies should conduct regular cybersecurity training for employees to inform them about identifying phishing attempts, such as scrutinizing email senders and links. Implementing multi-factor authentication (MFA) on company accounts could provide an additional layer of security for account access. IT teams should continuously monitor for unusual email activities and employ systems capable of simulating potential breach scenarios to test and adjust security defenses effectively.
