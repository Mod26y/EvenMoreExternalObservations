Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/microsoft-azure-monitor-alerts-abused-in-callback-phishing-campaigns/

**1) What happened?**

A callback phishing campaign has emerged, exploiting Microsoft Azure Monitor alerts to send deceptive emails, purportedly from the Microsoft Security Team, about unauthorized charges on user accounts. These emails appear authentic as they are sent from the legitimate email address azure-noreply@microsoft.com, thereby passing standard email security checks like SPF, DKIM, and DMARC. The threat actors create alerts in Azure Monitor using easily triggered billing conditions and insert their phishing message in the description, urging recipients to call a phone number for urgent clarification, potentially leading to credential theft or fraud.

**2) Why it matters?**

This development is significant because it leverages the credibility of Microsoft's own platforms, thus increasing the likelihood of phishing success. By utilizing legitimate channels, these attacks bypass traditional security filters, leading to a heightened risk for users to fall victim, especially within enterprise environments. The campaign not only threatens individual users but could also compromise entire corporate networks, providing attackers with initial access for broader, more devastating intrusions. Its ability to evade detection highlights vulnerabilities even in well-established security frameworks.

**3) What actions should be taken?**

Organizations should increase awareness among employees regarding such phishing techniques, urging them to be cautious with alerts containing phone numbers or urgent billing issue resolutions. It's advisable to configure additional verification steps for financial alerts and encourage direct verification of charges through independent account logins. Security teams could employ advanced threat detection tools to identify and block potentially harmful emails or calls. Regular training and simulated phishing exercises could enhance employee vigilance. Additionally, reviewing and possibly restricting Azure Monitor alert configurations to prevent exploitation by threat actors may be beneficial.
