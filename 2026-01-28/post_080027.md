Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/fortinet-blocks-exploited-forticloud-sso-zero-day-until-patch-is-ready/

The article details a critical zero-day vulnerability in Fortinet's FortiCloud SSO, tracked as CVE-2026-24858, that allows attackers to bypass authentication and gain administrative access to devices like FortiOS and others. Fortinet has temporarily mitigated this by disabling vulnerable FortiCloud SSO connections while developing a patch. This flaw is especially concerning as it affects devices that are otherwise fully patched and may extend to other SAML-based SSO systems. Fortinet also detected attempts using malicious FortiCloud accounts, further complicating the trust in FortiCloud SSO's security framework.

This matter is significant because it exposes a critical component of Fortinet's infrastructure to potential unauthorized access and data breaches, jeopardizing organizational security. The vulnerability illustrates the persistent risk even in well-secured systems and highlights the challenges in securing Single Sign-On (SSO) mechanisms. If exploited, attackers could hijack accounts and extract sensitive configuration data, which may result in significant operational and reputational damage for the impacted entities. The broader implication that the vulnerability could affect other SAML-based SSO implementations suggests systemic vulnerabilities in similar authentication systems.

In response, organizations should restrict administrative access and consider disabling FortiCloud SSO until patches are issued to prevent exploitation. Regularly auditing administrator accounts and reinforcing access control measures would be prudent. Monitoring for indicators of compromise, such as suspicious IP connections or newly created admin accounts, is also crucial. Furthermore, backing up configurations and rotating credentials, especially for any accounts linked to FortiCloud access, would be sensible risk mitigation measures. Organizations should stay informed through advisories for any updates on patches or changes in the risk landscape concerning this flaw.
