The article discusses the new variant of the CoolClient backdoor used by the Chinese espionage group Mustang Panda, which includes infostealers to extract login data from web browsers and monitor clipboard data. Researchers from Kaspersky found it in attacks targeting governmental entities across Asia and Russia. This sophisticated malware version is implemented using legitimate software signatures, prolonging its undetectable stay in the system by sideloading DLLs and running encrypted files, with enhanced plugins facilitating intricate data theft and espionage activities through plugins for remote shell access and service management.

This development signifies escalating cyber threats from state-sponsored actors like Mustang Panda, capable of targeting critical infrastructures and governmental institutions.
