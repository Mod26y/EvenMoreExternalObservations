Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/fbi-seizes-domain-storing-bank-credentials-stolen-from-us-victims/

1) The FBI, with international assistance, seized the domain 'web3adspanels.org' used by cybercriminals to store stolen bank credentials from U.S. victims. These credentials were gathered through phishing campaigns involving fake banking portals accessed via fraudulent ads on Google and Bing. The operation resulted in real and attempted financial losses totaling approximately $28 million, affecting at least 19 victims, including businesses.

2) This event underscores the sophisticated techniques cybercriminals employ to execute financial fraud, highlighting vulnerabilities in online ad platforms that could redirect users to illegitimate sites. The scheme stresses the importance of robust cybersecurity measures and an alert online banking user base to prevent such fraudulent activities and mitigate significant financial impacts.

3) Following this incident, organizations are encouraged to enhance awareness programs educating employees and users about identifying fraudulent adverts. Implementing technology solutions like ad blockers and promoting the practice of navigating to banking portals through direct bookmarks can reduce the risk of credential theft. Continuous monitoring and collaboration with law enforcement can also be crucial in responding quickly to such threats and protecting sensitive data.
