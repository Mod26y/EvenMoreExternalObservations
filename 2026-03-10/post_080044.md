**1) What happened:**

Ericsson Inc., the U.S. branch of the telecommunications company Ericsson, experienced a data breach through its service provider, exposing the personal data of employees and customers. The breach was active between April 17 and April 22, 2025, with the investigation revealing unauthorized access to data. Although the incident was discovered on April 28, 2025, the affected personal data, including names, Social Security Numbers, and financial details, was not confirmed until the completion of an investigation on February 23, 2026. So far, there's no evidence of misuse, and Ericsson is offering identity protection services to those affected.

**2) Why it matters:**

This incident underscores the vulnerabilities inherent in third-party service providers, which can act as entry points for cyber attackers. The breach impacted not only Ericsson's reputation but also potentially compromised customer and employee trust due to the exposure of highly sensitive data, including financial information and identity documents. The lack of an identified perpetrator adds complexity to the incident, raising questions about ransom payment or undiscovered deliberate actions. This case highlights the critical need for stringent security measures across all partners in an organization’s supply chain.

**3) What actions should be taken:**

Organizations should evaluate and enhance their third-party risk management frameworks, ensuring service providers adhere to robust cybersecurity standards. Continuous monitoring of third-party systems for vulnerabilities and potential breaches is crucial. Education and training initiatives should be strengthened for employees to recognize possible threats. Additionally, companies might consider investing in advanced breach detection and response technologies. Ericsson's provision of identity protection services post-breach is a sound initiative that should be considered by other organizations as a proactive step to safeguard affected individuals following a breach.
