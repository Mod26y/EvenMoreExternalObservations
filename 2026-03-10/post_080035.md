Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/dutch-govt-warns-of-signal-whatsapp-account-hijacking-attacks/

1) Russian state-sponsored hackers are conducting phishing campaigns targeting Signal and WhatsApp users, particularly government officials, military personnel, and journalists, to hijack their accounts. The hackers impersonate support services to trick users into providing verification codes and PINs, allowing them to realize unauthorized access. This attack strategy also involves QR codes that covertly link the attacker’s device to the victim’s account.

2) This matter highlights the vulnerabilities even within secure communication apps used by high-profile individuals, posing risks to sensitive communications and data integrity. Such attacks can lead to unauthorized access, data leaks, and identity impersonation, thereby impacting national security, individual privacy, and operational confidentiality.

3) Users could enhance their security practices by being cautious about unsolicited communication requests, not sharing verification codes or PINs, and regularly auditing linked devices on messaging apps. Organizations may consider training their staff on identifying social engineering and phishing attempts, implementing multi-factor authentication where possible, and using verified channels for communications involving sensitive data or instructions. These actions contribute to a more secure communication environment and mitigate potential threats.
