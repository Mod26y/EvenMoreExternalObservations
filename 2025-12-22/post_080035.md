Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/microsoft-365-accounts-targeted-in-wave-of-oauth-phishing-attacks/

1) The phishing attack targeting Microsoft 365 accounts uses OAuth device code authorization, where users are tricked into entering a device code on Microsoft's legitimate login page. This grants attackers access to accounts without stealing credentials or bypassing MFA. The attacks have increased since September, involving financially motivated and state-aligned threat actors. Different phishing kits simplify the process, and various campaigns, including those promising salary bonuses or spoofing OneDrive, have been documented. 

2) This issue is significant as it combines legitimate login workflows with advanced social engineering, bypassing traditional security measures like MFA. The prevalence and increasing sophistication of such phishing attacks pose a substantial risk, particularly to sensitive sectors such as government and academia. These sectors face potential data breaches leading to espionage or financial loss. Being aware of and responding to these attack techniques is crucial for any organization using Microsoft 365.

3) Organizations should consider implementing Microsoft's Entra Conditional Access for enhanced control over sign-in origination and devices used to access corporate accounts. Continuous user education on recognizing phishing attempts and the nature of these OAuth attacks is vital. Additionally, investing in advanced email filtering solutions to detect and mitigate phishing attempts can mitigate exposure to such threats. Regularly reviewing and updating security protocols according to evolving threats enhances overall cybersecurity posture.
