Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/whatsapp-device-linking-abused-in-account-hijacking-attacks/

1) What happened: A cybersecurity threat known as GhostPairing has emerged, exploiting WhatsApp's legitimate device-linking feature. Attackers convince victims to link the attackers' browser to their WhatsApp account without requiring authentication by sending deceptive messages that impersonate known contacts. This social engineering attack results in full access to the victim's conversation history and shared media, allowing the attackers to impersonate users or commit fraud. The initial campaign was discovered in Czechia, but the method could easily spread to other regions, leveraging compromised accounts to target additional victims.

2) Why it matters: This hijacking technique exploits trusting behaviors by using spoofed messages from known contacts, making it particularly insidious and hard to detect. As the attacker gains comprehensive access to the victim's WhatsApp data, they can perform fraudulent activities undetected until significant damage is done. This vulnerability specifically in WhatsApp's device linking poses significant risks to user privacy, trust, and security, emphasizing the importance of awareness and proactive security practices among users globally.

3) What actions should be taken: Users should become vigilant about recognizing suspicious messages and links, especially those prompting for actions like entering codes or credentials. Activating WhatsApp's two-factor authentication is crucial to add an additional layer of security. Regularly checking the Linked Devices section of the WhatsApp settings can help identify and remove unauthorized access. Educating users about such scams, encouraging scrutiny of unexpected messages, and fostering a security-aware culture are essential steps in mitigating the risk of such attacks. Additionally, organizations should ensure their cybersecurity protocols include response strategies for potential WhatsApp threats.
