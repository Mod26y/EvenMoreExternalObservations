Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/zeroday-cloud-hacking-event-awards-320-0000-for-11-zero-days/

1) What happened: The Zeroday Cloud hacking event held in London awarded $320,000 to security researchers for discovering 11 zero-day vulnerabilities affecting various cloud systems. The first competition of its kind, it was hosted by Wiz Research, collaborating with Amazon Web Services, Microsoft, and Google Cloud. Researchers succeeded in 85% of their hacking attempts across 13 sessions, identifying critical vulnerabilities in cloud infrastructure components like Redis, PostgreSQL, Grafana, and the Linux kernel. The Linux kernel vulnerability allowed a container escape, compromising tenant isolation. While AI systems were targeted, no successful exploits were demonstrated.

2) Why it matters: This competition underscores inherent security challenges within cloud environments, particularly those posed by zero-day vulnerabilities. Identifying and awarding these exploits highlights the ongoing need to strengthen cloud security measures, given the critical nature of the data and services these platforms support. The exposure of vulnerabilities in widely-used databases and kernels poses significant risks to business operations and data integrity. Moreover, the high rate of successful attacks emphasizes the capacity of skilled researchers to find novel vulnerabilities, stressing the importance of proactive security investments.

3) What actions should be taken: Organizations utilizing cloud services, especially those employing Redis, PostgreSQL, Grafana, and Linux-based systems, should prioritize patch management and consider threat intelligence services to stay informed about emerging threats. It's valuable to engage in regular security assessments and consider bug bounty programs to encourage the discovery and remediation of vulnerabilities before they can be exploited. Training developers on secure coding practices and investing in advanced monitoring tools can help identify potential breaches. Additionally, fostering partnerships with cybersecurity communities and events can enhance an organization's preparedness against future threats.
