Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/critical-flaw-in-wolfssl-library-enables-forged-certificate-use/

The article reports a critical vulnerability in the wolfSSL library, used for SSL/TLS encryption, which affects the verification of ECDSA signatures. This flaw, tracked as CVE-2026-5194, enables attackers to use forged certificates by improperly verifying the hash algorithm or its size. As wolfSSL is embedded in over 5 billion devices, including IoT, industrial, and military equipment, this poses a significant security risk. The vulnerability was addressed in wolfSSL version 5.9.1.

This flaw has profound implications because it opens the door for attackers to masquerade as legitimate servers or users. In environments where wolfSSL is deployed, attackers could potentially intercept, alter, or otherwise compromise secure communications, leading to data breaches and unauthorized access to sensitive systems. This undermines the integrity and trust in devices that rely on wolfSSL for secured communications, especially given its deployment in critical infrastructure and sensitive applications.

In response to this vulnerability, organizations using wolfSSL should immediately review their systems for any instances of the affected library. It is crucial to update wolfSSL to version 5.9.1 or later to prevent exploitation. Awareness of vendor advisories is recommended, especially for systems relying on customized firmware or SDKs where standard update protocols might not apply. Further, systems administrators should audit their security configurations for gateways and applications impacted by this vulnerability to prevent potential exploits.
