Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/us-sanctions-russian-bulletproof-hosting-provider-media-land-over-ransomware-ties/

**1) What happened:**
The article reports on the recent sanctions imposed by the United States, United Kingdom, and Australia on Russian bulletproof hosting (BPH) providers, particularly targeting Media Land and its associated entities. BPH providers are notorious for leasing servers to cybercriminals and ignoring law enforcement takedown requests, facilitating activities like ransomware, phishing, and DDoS attacks. Media Land and related companies supposedly offered services to ransomware groups, including LockBit and BlackSuit, and are now sanctioned alongside other companies and executives involved in similar activities.

**2) Why it matters:**
The sanctions highlight the international effort to combat cybercrime at a systemic level by disrupting vital infrastructure used by cybercriminals. Bulletproof hosting providers are crucial for the functioning of ransomware operations and other cyber threats, offering them a shield against law enforcement. By targeting these providers, the involved nations aim to cripple the ability of ransomware groups to operate unabated, thereby protecting businesses and critical national infrastructure, and signaling a strong stance against cybercriminal networks that threaten global security.

**3) What actions should be taken as a result of this information:**
Organizations should consider strengthening their cybersecurity posture by incorporating threat intelligence focused on bulletproof hosting providers' infrastructure. They should regularly update security measures, integrating high-confidence threat feeds, and conduct frequent traffic analysis to detect potential threats. Collaboration with internet service providers for customer notifications on identified malicious resources and enforcing stringent identity verification for new clients can also mitigate risks. Additionally, organizations could review their data backup and recovery plans to ensure resilience against potential ransomware attacks.
