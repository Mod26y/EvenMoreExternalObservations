2) Vendor risk management is crucial because third-party breaches can lead to data loss, reputation damage, and financial penalties for organizations. With increasing reliance on third-party services, understanding and mitigating these risks has become a significant part of maintaining a robust cybersecurity framework. Effective vendor risk management ensures that organizations protect their sensitive information from external threats and maintain trust with their stakeholders.

3) Organizations could focus on conducting thorough due diligence before engaging vendors, which includes evaluating their security policies and past performance. Regular monitoring and audits can help ensure ongoing compliance with security standards. Establishing clear contractual requirements for security measures and incident response plans can also mitigate risks. Training employees to recognize potential vendor risks and encouraging open communication can further reinforce an organization's overall security posture.
