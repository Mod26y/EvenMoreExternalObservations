Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/we-built-a-vulnerability-vending-machine-ai-tokens-in-zero-days-out/

**What happened:**  
Intruder developed an AI-driven pipeline to automate the discovery and exploitation of vulnerabilities in software, demonstrating its capability by identifying a SQL injection zero-day in a popular WordPress plugin used by over 300,000 users. This system utilizes Large Language Models (LLMs) alongside traditional code scanning frameworks to effectively pinpoint and exploit vulnerabilities. Specifically, they found a vulnerability (CVE-2026-3985) that affects installations using both the Creative Mail and WooCommerce plugins, allowing attackers to access sensitive database information through multi-stage requests.

**Why it matters:**  
This advancement highlights the ongoing evolution in cybersecurity tools, showing that AI can potentially match or exceed the capabilities of human vulnerability researchers. As AI-fueled discovery and exploitation tactics are refined, the time between vulnerability identification and exploitation shortens, increasing the risk of breaches. This development implies a future where both attackers and defenders might have access to rapid and powerful exploitation tools, challenging traditional defenses and possibly rendering them obsolete.

**What actions should be taken:**  
Organizations should consider integrating advanced AI-driven vulnerability scanning into their security strategies to proactively identify and patch vulnerabilities before they are exploited. Regularly updating security measures to incorporate the latest advancements in detection technology can help mitigate the risks associated with rapidly discovered vulnerabilities. Additionally, the security community should focus on developing robust AI-countermeasures to adapt to the evolving landscape where attackers might also utilize AI-driven tools. Specifically, users of affected plugins should be immediately informed to disable the Creative Mail plugin if used with WooCommerce and monitor for further updates or patches to secure their systems.
