Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/order-tracking-app-shop-abused-to-push-callback-phishing-attacks/

1) The cybersecurity incident involves the abuse of the Shop app, a popular order-tracking tool by Shopify, where threat actors insert fraudulent purchase receipts. These fake receipts imitate well-known brands such as Norton and Apple, tricking users into contacting bogus support numbers. The callers, pretending to be support agents, then use social engineering tactics to extract sensitive information like payment details or trick users into installing remote access software.

2) This development is significant as it represents a sophisticated evolution in phishing attacks known as "callback phishing." By leveraging the legitimacy and user trust in the Shop app, perpetrators increase the likelihood of their scam succeeding. The scale is concerning due to the app's widespread use, with millions of users potentially exposed. The incident also underscores the vulnerabilities within applications that integrate data from multiple sources without robust verification measures.

3) Organizations should enhance awareness campaigns to educate users about the potential risks of callback phishing through trusted apps. It is crucial to advise users to independently verify large purchase alerts by contacting their financial institutions directly rather than using information from potentially fake receipts. Moreover, Shopify and similar platforms could improve security controls to detect and prevent unauthorized manipulation of order histories. Security teams should review and simulate similar attack scenarios to ensure effective detection and response mechanisms are in place.
