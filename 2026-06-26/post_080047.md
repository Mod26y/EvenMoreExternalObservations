Original Article: https://www.darkreading.com/cyberattacks-data-breaches/edtech-attackers-shift-schools-software-suppliers

1) What happened: Recent analysis indicates a shift in cyberattack strategies targeting the educational technology (EdTech) sector. Instead of directly attacking schools, cybercriminals are now increasingly focusing on the software suppliers serving educational institutions. This approach allows attackers to potentially compromise multiple schools by exploiting vulnerabilities within a single software provider.

2) Why it matters: This shift in strategy is significant because it increases the scale and impact of cyberattacks on the educational sector. By targeting software suppliers, attackers can gain access to sensitive data from multiple schools simultaneously, posing a broader systemic risk. The educational institutions rely heavily on these vendors for digital learning tools, making the vendors critical nodes that need robust protections.

3) What actions should be taken: Schools and EdTech providers might consider reevaluating their cybersecurity postures, especially around third-party risk management. Conducting thorough security assessments of vendor solutions and ensuring robust incident response plans are in place could be pivotal. Additionally, encouraging collaboration between schools and vendors for regular auditing and updating of security protocols can help mitigate risks posed by these evolving cyber threats.
