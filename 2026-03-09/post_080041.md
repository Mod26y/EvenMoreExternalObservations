Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/cognizant-trizetto-breach-exposes-health-data-of-34-million-patients/

1) The TriZetto Provider Solutions, part of Cognizant, experienced a data breach affecting 3.4 million individuals' personal and health information. Suspicious activity was detected on October 2, 2025, but investigation revealed the breach started on November 19, 2024. The compromised data includes names, Social Security numbers, and other identifiers, but not financial information. While TriZetto has enhanced its cybersecurity and notified affected parties, questions remain regarding notification delays.

2) This breach highlights vulnerabilities in healthcare IT systems, showcasing the potential risks to patient information that can stem from prolonged unauthorized access. Since healthcare data is particularly sensitive due to its personal nature, exposures can lead to significant privacy violations and potential misuse, affecting patient trust and drawing regulatory scrutiny. The delays in notification also bring to light the importance of timely communication in mitigating potential fallout.

3) Organizations should conduct thorough risk assessments and continuously monitor for unauthorized activities to detect breaches promptly. Regular audits and updates to security protocols can help protect sensitive data. Improved incident response plans, ensuring rapid mitigation and transparent communication, can minimize the impact of any breaches. Offering credit and identity protection services can help affected individuals protect themselves from possible identity theft, and collaboration with law enforcement can aid in tracing threats.
