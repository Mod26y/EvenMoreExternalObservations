Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/microsoft-hackers-abusing-ai-at-every-stage-of-cyberattacks/

1) Microsoft reports that threat actors are increasingly leveraging artificial intelligence (AI) throughout the lifecycle of cyberattacks. AI is being used by these actors to draft phishing emails, generate infrastructure, create malware, and maintain access through false identities. Notably, groups like Jasper Sleet use AI to forge digital personas for infiltrating organizations. Generative AI is serving as a "force multiplier," making attacks faster and reducing technical complexity, while some threat actors are experimenting with AI systems that can perform tasks autonomously.

2) This matters because it demonstrates how AI is accelerating and amplifying cyber threats, making them more sophisticated and accessible to a wider range of actors. The integration of AI allows for more efficient and convincing social engineering, and more adaptive and resilient malware. This trend indicates a growing need for enhanced defenses and policies to counteract the increased scale and pace of this evolving threat landscape. Organizations must recognize AI as both a tool and a target in cybersecurity.

3) Organizations should focus on strengthening their cybersecurity posture against AI-driven attacks by enhancing identity protection measures and detecting abnormal credential use. Key actions may include improving phishing detection systems, hardening identity systems, and monitoring for atypical activities that suggest insider risks or compromised access. Continuous training and education on AI-driven threats should be provided to bolster security awareness among employees. Additionally, securing AI systems and ensuring robust safeguards can mitigate risks from both external threats and internal misuse of AI technologies.
