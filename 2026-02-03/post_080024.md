Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/russian-hackers-exploit-recently-patched-microsoft-office-bug-in-attacks/

The article discusses a cybersecurity incident where Russian hackers, specifically the well-known group APT28 (Fancy Bear), exploited a newly patched zero-day vulnerability in Microsoft Office (CVE-2026-21509). These hackers used malicious DOC files, themed around EU and Ukrainian government topics, to distribute malware. This exploitation took place shortly after Microsoft released an emergency update addressing the vulnerability. The attack involves tricking users into downloading malware, which then compromises systems through various techniques such as COM hijacking and scheduled tasks, ultimately facilitating remote access for threat actors.

This incident matters because it highlights the significant threat posed by state-sponsored actors exploiting security vulnerabilities shortly after their disclosure. Such rapid exploitation underscores the need for timely application of security updates, as delays can lead to severe compromises, especially in sensitive sectors like government organizations. Moreover, since the attack impacts both Ukrainian and EU-based entities, it indicates an escalation in the geographical scope and target diversity of cyber operations related to geopolitical conflicts.

To address this risk, organizations should prioritize the immediate application of the latest security patches for Microsoft Office products as recommended by CERT-UA. Implementing registry-based mitigations may serve as a temporary measure if patching cannot be applied immediately. Monitoring and potentially blocking connections to COVENANT’s command-and-control cloud service would enhance defenses against this threat. Additionally, enabling Defender’s Protected View provides an extra layer of protection by restricting untrusted Office files from executing malicious operations.
