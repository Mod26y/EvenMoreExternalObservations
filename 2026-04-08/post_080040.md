Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/us-warns-of-iranian-hackers-targeting-critical-infrastructure/

The article reports on a warning from US agencies about Iranian-linked hackers targeting Internet-exposed Rockwell/Allen-Bradley PLCs on U.S. critical infrastructure networks. These cyberattacks have focused on disrupting various critical sectors like government services, water systems, and energy since March 2026. The cyber actors have manipulated data, intending to cause disruptions, as tensions between Iran, the U.S., and Israel escalate. The reported attacks show the evolving threat landscape aimed at nation-state critical technologies, highlighting significant vulnerabilities that could compromise safety and security.

This situation is critical because it highlights the vulnerabilities in U.S. critical infrastructure, emphasizing the potential for Iranian state-affiliated actors to cause severe disruptions. As PLCs are integral to managing industrial operations, malicious interference could lead to substantial operational and financial losses. Furthermore, this development demonstrates the broader geopolitical cyber threat landscape, underscoring the importance of securing essential technologies against emerging threats that could affect public safety and operational continuity.

Organizations affected by these threats should consider several proactive measures. Disconnecting PLCs from the Internet or securing them via firewalls is recommended, alongside consistent vulnerability scanning and monitoring for suspicious traffic. Other actions include implementing multifactor authentication, regular firmware updates, and disabling unused services. Enhancing network traffic monitoring and ensuring detailed logging will further aid in real-time threat detection and mitigation, bolstering the resilience of critical infrastructure against sophisticated cyberattacks.
