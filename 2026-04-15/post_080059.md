1) While the specific content of the article is inaccessible, the title indicates a discussion about the importance of preparing networks to withstand Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) attacks, especially during peak traffic periods. DDoS attacks aim to overwhelm an organization's network with malicious traffic, causing service interruptions or complete outages. Testing network resilience is crucial as it helps organizations identify vulnerabilities and strengthen their defenses to ensure availability and performance, even during peak loads or attacks.

2) Understanding and mitigating DDoS threats is critical due to their potential to disrupt essential services, result in financial losses, and damage reputations. High-traffic periods make networks more susceptible to such attacks due to the already elevated load, which attackers can exploit to maximize disruption. Strengthening network security against DDoS attacks ensures that customer trust remains intact, services are consistently available, and business operations continue smoothly, particularly during critical peak usage times.

3) Organizations should regularly conduct stress tests on their networks simulating DDoS attacks to assess and enhance their defenses. Developing robust incident response strategies, integrating load balancers, and leveraging cloud-based DDoS protection services can significantly increase resilience. Moreover, collaborating with ISPs for traffic filtering and fostering a culture of awareness among staff are beneficial. By building redundancy into their systems and continuously monitoring traffic patterns, organizations can better anticipate and mitigate the risks associated with DDoS attacks during peak operational periods.
