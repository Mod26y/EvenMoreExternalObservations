**1) What happened:**

The article highlights the failure of backup systems during ransomware attacks, even when backups are present. This failure occurs because attackers often target backup systems, rendering them exposed, accessible, and unprotected, ultimately resulting in data recovery becoming impossible. The attackers typically follow a sequence from gaining initial access, stealing credentials, moving laterally, discovering, and destroying backups before deploying ransomware. This process exposes the vulnerability of backup systems that are not properly isolated or protected with security controls, leading to a failure in recovery during ransomware incidents.

**2) Why it matters:**

This information is crucial as it shatters the conventional belief that having backups guarantees recovery from ransomware attacks. In reality, if backups are not fortified with security measures like immutability, access protection, and threat detection, they can become a single point of failure. With ransomware attacks continuing to rise in frequency and severity, understanding these vulnerabilities is essential for organizations to safeguard their data and ensure business continuity in the face of such threats.

**3) What actions should be taken:**

Organizations should adopt a resilience-first approach to backup strategies by incorporating security measures such as immutability, multifactor authentication, and strict access controls. Backups must be isolated from production systems and regularly monitored for anomalous activities. Implementing comprehensive solutions like the Acronis Cyber Platform, which integrates backup with cybersecurity, can enhance visibility and streamline response actions. Consistently testing recovery processes and preparing for attack scenarios can further strengthen organizational defenses against ransomware threats. Ultimately, backups should be resilient enough to withstand attacks, ensuring reliable recovery at all times.
