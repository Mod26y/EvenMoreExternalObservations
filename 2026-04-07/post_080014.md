Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/german-authorities-identify-revil-and-gangcrab-ransomware-bosses/

**1) What happened:** German authorities have identified two Russian nationals, Daniil Maksimovich Shchukin and Anatoly Sergeevitsch Kravchuk, as the leaders of the GandCrab and REvil ransomware operations between 2019 and 2021. Shchukin, known by the alias UNKN/UNKNOWN, and Kravchuk were linked to 130 extortion cases in Germany, extracting $2.2 million in ransom payments from at least 25 victims, with total damages exceeding $40 million. The BKA has released images and other identifiers to aid in their capture, despite their current presumed location in Russia.

**2) Why it matters:** The identification of high-level perpetrators behind notorious ransomware groups like GandCrab and REvil signifies a significant step in international cybersecurity enforcement. Given the extensive financial impact and high-profile nature of these operations, it reinforces the necessity for international cooperation in cybersecurity. These groups have targeted crucial infrastructure, such as multiple Texas local governments and the massive Kaseya supply-chain attack affecting around 1,500 victims, emphasizing the threat ransomware poses to cybersecurity worldwide.

**3) What actions should be taken:** Organizations should enhance their cybersecurity defenses by adopting comprehensive threat intelligence, monitoring, and incident response capabilities. They should also participate in information sharing with law enforcement and global cybersecurity communities to quickly address emerging threats. Public and private sectors should collaborate to develop more resilient infrastructures and consider incorporating advanced practices like zero trust architecture to mitigate the impacts of ransomware attacks. Supporting international law enforcement efforts by reporting suspicious activities can aid in tracking and capturing cybercriminals.
