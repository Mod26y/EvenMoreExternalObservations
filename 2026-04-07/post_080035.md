Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/microsoft/microsoft-fixes-classic-outlook-bug-causing-email-delivery-issues/

1) What happened:
Microsoft resolved an issue that affected Classic Outlook users, which prevented them from sending emails through Outlook.com. This problem had been affecting users whose Outlook.com accounts were linked to another Exchange account, generating non-delivery reports with specific error messages. The company fixed the issue with a server-side update implemented on April 3, 2026. For users still affected, Microsoft suggests using the New Outlook client or web-based Outlook.com as alternatives. 

2) Why it matters:
Email remains a critical communication tool for organizations, and disruptions in email delivery can significantly impact operational efficiency and customer interactions. The resolution of this bug is crucial for maintaining reliable communication systems and user trust in Microsoft's services. Furthermore, understanding the cause of such issues helps inform better management of linked accounts and troubleshooting procedures, which can be crucial for preventing future disruptions.

3) What actions should be taken:
Organizations utilizing Classic Outlook should inform their IT departments of this issue and the subsequent fix. IT teams might guide users to switch to the New Outlook client or Outlook.com temporarily if they experience email delivery issues. It's also advisable to monitor Microsoft's updates and support documents for any further instructions or pending issues. Additionally, maintaining awareness of emerging bugs and solutions helps ensure proactive management of IT resources and minimizes disruption impact.
