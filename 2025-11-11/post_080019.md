Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/mozilla-firefox-gets-new-anti-fingerprinting-defenses/

1. **What happened:** Mozilla released Firefox version 145 with enhanced anti-fingerprinting defenses primarily available in Private Browsing and Enhanced Tracking Protection Strict modes. These phased protections aim to reduce digital fingerprinting, a method of tracking users through subtle device and session identifiers. Techniques involve altering how graphics, fonts, and system details are reported to reduce trackability from 35% to 20%. Initial protections will be tested before becoming default settings in Firefox, and users can opt-out if necessary.

2. **Why it matters:** This update is significant in bolstering user privacy and security, reducing the risk of targeted tracking even when other measures like cookie-blocking are active. Fingerprinting can undermine privacy by uniquely identifying users across multiple browsing sessions, potentially leading to targeted ads or more severe privacy invasion. By minimizing fingerprinting effectiveness, Mozilla enhances user trust and aligns with growing public and regulatory demands for heightened digital privacy protections.

3. **What actions should be taken:** Users should be encouraged to utilize Firefox 145's Enhanced Tracking Protection and Private Browsing modes to take advantage of these updated protections. Businesses and IT departments should test websites for compatibility with these changes and educate employees about digital fingerprinting risks. Agencies can also consider this a catalyst to audit other browser-based tracking practices, ensuring alignment with privacy protocols and promoting awareness around reducing fingerprinting vulnerabilities.
