Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/apple-account-change-alerts-abused-to-send-phishing-emails/

The article explains that Apple account change notifications are being exploited to send phishing emails from Apple's own servers. The attackers insert phishing messages into Apple ID personal fields, sending legitimate-looking emails that claim an iPhone purchase was made via PayPal. These emails include a fake support phone number, attempting to deceive recipients into making contact. This tactic is effective because it cleverly uses Apple's legitimate communication channels, passing security checks and bypassing spam filters, making the scam harder to detect.

This matters because it highlights a sophisticated phishing technique that leverages the reputation and infrastructure of a trusted entity like Apple to target users. Users are more likely to trust emails that appear to originate from recognizable services, increasing the risk of individuals falling for the scam. Furthermore, the use of legitimate delivery methods, combined with authentication passings (such as SPF, DKIM, and DMARC), can make these phishing emails particularly challenging for conventional security tools to detect and block.

To mitigate the impact of such attacks, organizations should educate users about the nature of these phishing scams and advise them to be cautious of unexpected purchase notifications and requests for personal information or calls to unknown numbers. Implementing advanced email filtering technologies capable of analyzing patterns and behaviors, rather than solely relying on authentication checks, could help identify and block such phishing attempts. Engaging with companies like Apple to encourage processes that minimize potential abuse of their systems would also be beneficial.
