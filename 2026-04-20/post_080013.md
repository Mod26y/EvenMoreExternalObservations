Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/vercel-confirms-breach-as-hackers-claim-to-be-selling-stolen-data/

**1) What happened?**

Vercel, a cloud development platform, experienced a security breach stemming from a compromised Google Workspace account due to a breach at Context.ai, a third-party AI tool. Attackers accessed Vercel's internal systems and began selling supposed stolen data on hacking forums, including alleged access keys, source codes, database data, and employee information. Vercel confirmed the breach impacted a limited set of customers and is actively investigating alongside law enforcement. The initial unauthorized access was exacerbated by enumerating environment variables not marked as sensitive, allowing further access to potential sensitive systems.

**2) Why it matters?**

This breach highlights vulnerabilities within interconnected systems and emphasizes the ripple impact of third-party breaches on primary services. Vercel serves numerous developers and companies using JavaScript frameworks, making the potential exposure of access keys, API tokens, and internal data a significant security risk. It also underscores the importance of encrypting environment variables, as their accessibility can facilitate broader exploitation. For county and organizational IT leaders, this incident serves as a reminder of the dangers of non-secure storage practices and the potential consequences of weak identity management systems.

**3) What actions should be taken as a result of this information?**

Organizations using Vercel should immediately review and rotate their environment variables, ensuring the use of Vercel's sensitive environment variable feature for enhanced security. It may also be beneficial to perform a security audit to identify any potential vulnerabilities linked to third-party services and consider bolstering their incident response plans. Since the attackers exploited access through an OAuth compromise, strengthening authentication measures, including implementing multi-factor authentication and regular access audits, could deter similar breaches. Additionally, organizations should follow Vercel’s updates and advisories closely for new prevention and remediation strategies.
