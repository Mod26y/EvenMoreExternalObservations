Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/microsoft/microsoft-teams-right-click-paste-broken-by-edge-update-bug/

1. **What Happened:** A recent update to the Microsoft Edge browser caused a code regression that affected the functionality of the Microsoft Teams desktop client. Specifically, the bug prevents users from using the right-click context menu to paste URLs, text, or images in Teams chats, rendering the "Paste" option greyed out. However, users can still use keyboard shortcuts to copy and paste content. Microsoft has identified the source of the issue and is rolling out a fix in stages, while closely monitoring system telemetry to ensure recovery.

2. **Why It Matters:** This issue highlights the potential disruption caused by software updates and their unintended consequences in both corporate and individual environments that rely on Microsoft Teams for communication. Pasting through the right-click menu is a common action, and its disruption can lead to decreased productivity and frustration among users. The situation underscores the importance of thorough testing and swift problem resolution for software updates, especially for widely used applications like Microsoft Teams, which play a crucial role in day-to-day business operations.

3. **What Actions Should be Taken:** Administrators and users should continue to use keyboard shortcuts as a temporary workaround for pasting content in Teams. Additionally, IT departments should stay informed about updates from Microsoft regarding the fix's rollout and prepare to communicate any changes or instructions to their user base. Monitoring Microsoft's advisories and forums can provide early awareness of other potential disruptions. Organizations may also consider establishing a protocol for rapidly identifying and responding to software issues resulting from updates to minimize impact on business operations.
