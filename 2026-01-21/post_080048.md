Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/acf-plugin-bug-gives-hackers-admin-on-50-000-wordpress-sites/

**1) What happened:**
A critical vulnerability, identified as CVE-2025-14533, was discovered in the ACF Extended plugin for WordPress, affecting versions 0.9.2.1 and earlier. This flaw allows unauthenticated attackers to gain administrative rights on sites by exploiting the "Insert User / Update User" form action, compromising the site's security. Although the plugin fix was released promptly within four days of identifying the issue, approximately 50,000 sites remain vulnerable due to the potential exposure from previous plugin downloads and incomplete patching.

**2) Why it matters:**
The vulnerability poses significant risks due to the potential for complete site takeovers by malicious actors. Given that administrative access allows for control over site content, user data, and further exploitation of the network, this flaw could lead to extensive data breaches and reputational damage. Additionally, the wide deployment of WordPress makes it a lucrative target for attackers, underscoring the need for vigilance in patch management and security updates.

**3) What actions should be taken:**
Administrators should immediately verify the version of ACF Extended installed on their WordPress sites and update to version 0.9.2.2 or newer to mitigate the vulnerability. Regular security audits and monitoring for suspicious user creation activities are also recommended to detect and respond to potential exploitation. Furthermore, staying informed about security advisories and subscribing to threat intelligence feeds can help in promptly addressing any new vulnerabilities. Implementing robust access controls and utilizing web application firewalls can provide additional layers of security.
