Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/oracle-mitigates-peoplesoft-zero-day-exploited-in-data-theft-attacks/

1) The incident involves a critical zero-day vulnerability, tracked as CVE-2026-35273, in Oracle PeopleSoft PeopleTools that allows unauthenticated remote code execution. The flaw, with a CVSS score of 9.8, is actively exploited in data theft attacks by the ShinyHunters extortion gang. These attackers breached over 100 organizations, including education sector entities, stealing data by leveraging old and zero-day vulnerabilities. Oracle has issued emergency mitigations and plans to release a patch. Mandiant's investigation revealed that ShinyHunters used specific IP addresses to exploit the flaw, conducting reconnaissance and lateral movement within compromised systems.

2) This incident matters because it highlights a severe vulnerability in widely used enterprise software, posing a critical risk to organizations’ sensitive data and operations. Exploitation can lead to significant data breaches with potential extortion and financial losses, particularly in sectors like education that manage vast amounts of sensitive information. Furthermore, it underscores the persistent threat posed by sophisticated threat actors exploiting both new and existing vulnerabilities, necessitating ongoing vigilance and effective security measures among targeted organizations.

3) Organizations using Oracle PeopleSoft should promptly apply Oracle's emergency mitigations and prepare for the upcoming patch deployment. They should restrict access to vulnerable endpoints, review server logs for suspicious activity, and assess systems for compromise signs, including unexpected webshells or unauthorized files. Monitoring the shared IP addresses involved in the attacks could also aid detection. Implementing robust monitoring, utilizing security information and event management (SIEM) systems, and conducting regular security assessments can help in identifying potential vulnerabilities and strengthening defenses. Engaging in proactive threat intelligence sharing and awareness initiatives may also be beneficial.
