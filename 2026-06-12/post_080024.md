Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/maine-breach-portal-abused-to-publish-fake-data-breach-disclosures/

The article describes a misinformation incident where fake data breach disclosures were submitted and published on Maine's official breach portal before undergoing proper verification. An entry falsely claimed a security breach of VRChat, suggesting the exposure of personal data of over 2.4 million users. Similarly, a dubious notice purportedly from Discord was also submitted, despite factual inconsistencies. These incidents signify a flaw in the breach notification system, allowing misinformation to spread easily.

This situation highlights the critical importance of verification and vetting processes for data breach disclosures. The publication of fraudulent data breaches can lead to reputational damage for companies, unnecessary panic among consumers, and broader misinformation. It undermines trust in official breach notification portals, emphasizing the need for enhanced scrutiny to maintain the credibility of such platforms, especially when they serve as primary resources for informing the public about data security incidents.

As a response, enhancing submission verification protocols is essential to prevent the publication of false data breaches. The process could involve additional verification steps, such as direct confirmation from the purportedly affected company before accepting entries. Furthermore, educating consumers and journalists about the importance of verifying breach notifications directly with companies involved can help ensure that accurate information circulates. Adopting a multi-layered vetting process will protect against misinformation and maintain the integrity of data breach disclosures.
