Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/cisa-warns-of-actively-exploited-rce-flaws-in-joomla-extensions/

The U.S. Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA) has issued an alert regarding active exploitation of remote code execution vulnerabilities in Joomla extensions, specifically iCagenda and Balbooa Forms. These vulnerabilities allow unauthorized file uploads, potentially leading to data theft, web shell installation, and full website compromise. The flaws were exploited even before patches were released, highlighting the urgency and severity of the situation. 

This matter is critical because Joomla sites are widely used for various web applications, and exploitation of these flaws can lead to substantial security breaches. Successful attacks could result in the theft of sensitive data, disruption of services, and the unauthorized control of affected websites, posing significant risks to personal and organizational data security.

As a result of this information, Joomla site administrators should immediately identify if they are using the vulnerable iCagenda and Balbooa Forms extensions. It is crucial to update these plugins to the patched versions—iCagenda 4.0.8 or 3.9.15 and Balbooa Forms 2.4.1—as quickly as possible. Additionally, regular monitoring and auditing of site security should be conducted to ensure quick detection and mitigation of any potential exploitation attempts.
