Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/japans-largest-taxi-operator-shuts-systems-after-cyberattack/

Japan's largest taxi operator, Nihon Kotsu, experienced a significant cybersecurity incident, resulting in the compromise of its systems due to unauthorized external access, identified as a malware infection. This breach led the company to shut down affected systems immediately to prevent further damage. Key services, including taxi dispatch and reservation management systems, were disrupted. The incident affects many stakeholders, as the company employs over 18,000 people and operates a large fleet of vehicles.

This cyberattack underscores the potential vulnerability of critical infrastructure in a technologically advanced service sector like transportation. Nihon Kotsu's operational disruptions illustrate how such incidents can affect daily business operations and customer services, with downstream impacts on the local economy and community, especially when essential services are suspended, as seen with the "labor taxi" service for pregnant women. The potential data breach raises concerns about customer data security and trust.

Organizations, particularly in the transportation sector, should enhance their cybersecurity protocols, including regular penetration testing and incident response planning. Implementing stronger access controls and employee training on recognizing phishing attempts could mitigate such risks. Engaging external cybersecurity experts, as Nihon Kotsu did, is a crucial step in managing the aftermath of a breach, while transparent communication with customers about potential risks can help maintain trust. Organizations should remain vigilant for further information regarding potential data leaks, alerting customers appropriately.
