Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/okta-sso-accounts-targeted-in-vishing-based-data-theft-attacks/

**What happened:**  
Okta, a cloud-based identity provider, reported an increase in custom phishing kits designed for voice-based social engineering (vishing) attacks. These tools are utilized by multiple hacking groups in a service model aiming to steal Okta SSO credentials. The attackers manipulate targets in real-time by spoofed calls, creating phishing pages to capture credentials and MFA tokens, thus allowing unauthorized access to numerous enterprise platforms integrated with Okta.

**Why it matters:**  
The attacks highlight the significant risk posed by advanced phishing techniques that can circumvent standard security measures like multi-factor authentication (MFA). High-profile platforms accessed via Okta SSO, such as Microsoft 365 and Salesforce, hold sensitive business data, making them attractive targets for data theft and extortion. The impact spans beyond Okta users, threatening broader organizational cybersecurity frameworks and potentially leading to extensive data breaches.

**What actions should be taken:**  
Organizations are advised to adopt phishing-resistant MFA solutions, such as FIDO2 security keys, and enhance employee training on vigilance against sophisticated phishing methods. Conducting regular security audits and implementing strict access controls can help mitigate these risks. Additionally, staying informed about emerging threats and maintaining open communication with identity providers like Okta ensures organizations can swiftly adapt to evolving security landscapes.
