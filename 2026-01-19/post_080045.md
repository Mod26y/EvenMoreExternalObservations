Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/artificial-intelligence/google-chrome-tests-gemini-powered-ai-skills/

1) What happened: Google is actively testing "Skills" for its Gemini-AI within Chrome, which enables the AI to perform specific automated tasks. This emerging functionality expands Gemini's role from an AI "helper" to a more autonomous "agent," capable of tasks such as summarizing content, comparing information across tabs, and integrating tightly with Google apps. The new feature is accessible via a unique "chrome://skills" page where users can define specific tasks for Gemini to complete within their browser activities. While currently undergoing internal testing, the release timeframe to the public remains unspecified.

2) Why it matters: This development signifies a significant shift towards integrating advanced AI functionalities into commonly used web browsers, enhancing user efficiency and potentially transforming how tasks are managed online. By enabling personalized task automation and better integration with other Google services, it could streamline user interactions and improve productivity. However, this also raises important security and privacy concerns, as sophisticated AI functionalities might introduce potential vulnerabilities or privacy implications, especially if user data is leveraged to enhance AI capabilities.

3) What actions should be taken: Organizations should proactively evaluate the implications of such AI integrations on their IT security policies and infrastructure. Continuous monitoring for the rollout and testing of similar AI features will help organizations anticipate and prepare for potential vulnerabilities. Updating privacy policies to address AI capabilities and data usage, coupled with a robust training program for users on best practices and potential risks associated with AI-enhanced browsing, can mitigate risks. Additionally, coordination between IT and security teams will be crucial to assessing the impact of these browser features on the organization's security posture.
