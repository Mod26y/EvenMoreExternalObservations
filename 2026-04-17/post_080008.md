Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/microsoft/microsoft-warns-of-reboot-loops-affecting-some-domain-controllers/

1) The article describes an issue where Windows domain controllers are entering reboot loops due to crashes in the Local Security Authority Subsystem Service (LSASS) following the installation of the April 2026 security updates. This problem primarily affects non-Global Catalog domain controllers in environments using Privileged Access Management, disrupting authentication and directory services and potentially rendering the domain unavailable. The affected systems are predominantly various versions of Windows Server, including 2025, 2022, 23H2, 2019, and 2016.

2) This situation is critical as domain controllers are central components in managing network security and access. Frequent reboots and potential unavailability of these servers can lead to significant business disruption, including denied access to essential services and data. As the issue primarily impacts organizational environments rather than personal devices, it highlights the importance of robust update testing and management strategies within enterprise IT infrastructures, emphasizing the need for contingency plans when applying security patches.

3) In response to this information, IT departments should closely monitor communications from Microsoft regarding updates and hotfixes addressing this issue. Teams could establish a protocol for handling updates that involve critical components like domain controllers, including maintaining offline backups pre-update and testing patches in isolated environments first. Furthermore, organizations using Privileged Access Management should coordinate with Microsoft Support for Business to explore mitigation strategies to minimize impacts while a permanent fix is developed. Regularly reviewing and practicing incident response plans will also prepare IT teams to handle such disruptions efficiently.
