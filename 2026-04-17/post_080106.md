The duration indicates a sustained threat potentially exploiting similar vulnerabilities repeatedly due to insufficient protective measures in affected regions or sectors.

2) Why it matters: A six-year campaign targeting homes and SMBs highlights persistent cybersecurity vulnerabilities. SMBs, often with limited resources, are particularly vulnerable as they may lack robust security infrastructure. The threat to personal and business data can lead to financial losses, operational disruptions, and erosion of customer trust. The campaign's persistence signals a pressing need for improved cybersecurity measures and awareness, impacting how international regions perceive and prepare for these threats.

3) What actions should be taken: Stakeholders could increase awareness campaigns targeting vulnerable sectors, emphasizing cybersecurity best practices like regular software updates, offline data backups, and strong password policies. Supporting SMBs with resources for affordable cybersecurity tools and enhancing incident response frameworks could also prove beneficial. International cooperation and information sharing between cybersecurity entities might offer insights into threat patterns, enabling proactive defenses against ongoing and future ransomware activities.
