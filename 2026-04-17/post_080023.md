Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/recently-leaked-windows-zero-days-now-exploited-in-attacks/

1) What happened: Three critical zero-day exploits affecting Windows systems, known as BlueHammer, RedSun, and UnDefend, have been actively exploited. Researcher "Chaotic Eclipse" disclosed these vulnerabilities, with proof-of-concept code to protest Microsoft's handling of vulnerability disclosures. BlueHammer has received a patch, but RedSun and UnDefend remain unpatched. These zero-days allow attackers to gain elevated privileges and disrupt Windows Defender operations, compromising systems through local privilege escalation.

2) Why it matters: These exploits pose significant security risks to organizations using Windows systems, as they allow unauthorized elevation of privileges and disabling critical security defenses. This increases the potential for widespread attacks, data breaches, and persistent threat presence within networks. The exploits also highlight gaps in vulnerability disclosure and response processes at Microsoft, emphasizing the need for swift resolution and comprehensive protection strategies to safeguard sensitive data and maintain system integrity.

3) What actions should be taken: Organizations should prioritize immediate monitoring and detection of these exploits, particularly focusing on systems running Windows Defender. IT teams should apply the available patch for BlueHammer, while implementing temporary mitigation measures such as enhanced logging and network segmentation to reduce risk from the unpatched RedSun and UnDefend vulnerabilities. Developing an incident response plan to quickly address potential breaches is also recommended. Regularly review and test back-up and recovery processes, ensuring a robust posture against potential exploitation.
