Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/new-wave-of-vpn-login-attempts-targets-palo-alto-globalprotect-portals/

The article outlines a cyber threat campaign targeting Palo Alto GlobalProtect VPN portals and SonicWall SonicOS API endpoints. Originating from over 7,000 IP addresses mainly from Germany, the campaign involves brute force login attempts and scanning activities. This activity, attributed to a rogue actor, used client fingerprints previously associated with similar incidents. While no software vulnerabilities are exploited, these attempts indicate credential-based attacks, aiming to identify and exploit potential misconfigurations or vulnerabilities to compromise systems.

This campaign is significant due to its scope, the involvement of robust infrastructure from a known IT company, and its focus on widely-used security gateways in corporate and governmental environments. The actors' shift from VPN portals to firewall API endpoints underscores the evolving techniques cyber adversaries employ to breach networks. The prevalence of such attacks can pose severe risks if vulnerabilities or misconfigurations are discovered and exploited, potentially leading to unauthorized access or data breaches.

To mitigate these threats, organizations should reinforce their VPN and firewall security measures. Implementing Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) across user access points can significantly reduce the risk of credential abuse. Moreover, continuous monitoring of network activity for irregular login attempts and client fingerprints is crucial. Establishing a dynamic blocking mechanism to adapt to changing threat landscapes, alongside traditional IP blocking, can enhance defenses. Additionally, regular reviews and updates of configurations and endpoint security should be conducted to identify and rectify potential vulnerabilities.
