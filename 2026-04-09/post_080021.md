Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/hackers-use-pixel-large-svg-trick-to-hide-credit-card-stealer/

1) In this incident, a widespread hacking campaign has targeted nearly 100 online stores using the Magento platform. Hackers utilized a 1x1 pixel SVG image to conceal the credit card-stealing code, exploiting the PolyShell vulnerability found in Magento and Adobe Commerce installations. The attackers crafted a convincing overlay that captured payment details from legitimate users by exploiting a vulnerability allowing unauthorized code execution. Upon checkout, users were tricked into entering their payment details on a fake, “secure-looking” overlay, sending this sensitive information to the attackers.

2) This situation is critical because the Magento platform is widely used in e-commerce, meaning that a significant number of businesses and consumers are affected, potentially leading to financial losses and breaches of consumer trust. The ability to hide malicious code within seemingly harmless components like an SVG image complicates detection, underlining the sophistication of modern cyber threats. Furthermore, the delayed response by Adobe in addressing the PolyShell vulnerability exacerbates the risk, emphasizing the importance of timely patches.

3) In response to this threat, organizations using the Magento platform should proactively scan for hidden SVG tags with the 'onload' attribute containing the `atob()` function, indicative of malicious activity. Additionally, website administrators are advised to inspect their browser local storage for the `_mgx_cv` key, a signal of compromised payment data. Traffic to suspicious domains, including `/fb_metrics.php`, should be monitored and blocked, alongside known malicious IPs such as 23.137.249.67. Updating to the latest available Magento version, even if it is a beta release, may offer interim protection until a stable, patched version is released.
