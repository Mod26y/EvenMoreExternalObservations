Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/google-drive-ransomware-detection-now-on-by-default-for-paying-users/

The article reports that Google has made its AI-powered ransomware detection feature available to all paying Google Drive users by default. This system detects ransomware-encrypted files during file syncing from a desktop to Google Drive. It pauses sync to prevent further ransomware spread, notifies affected users and their IT administrators, and provides restoration tools to recover corrupted files.

This development is crucial as it enhances data protection for businesses, educational institutions, and individual users. Given the increasing sophistication and frequency of ransomware attacks, having a built-in, default detection system within Google Drive is significant for maintaining operational continuity and data integrity.

Organizations should ensure all endpoint devices use the latest version of Google Drive for desktop to receive detection alerts and manage incidents efficiently. IT administrators may also consider educating users on the system's capabilities and ensure they remain aware of potential threats, leveraging the available restoration tools in case of an attack. Organizations using multiple cloud services can look into similar features from Microsoft and Dropbox to align their cybersecurity strategies across platforms.
