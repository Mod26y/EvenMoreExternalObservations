Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/cisa-warns-of-cyberattacks-targeting-fuel-tank-monitoring-systems/

1) What happened: The Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA), together with several US government bodies, has issued a warning regarding cyberattacks targeting automatic tank gauge (ATG) systems used in critical infrastructure sectors. Hackers are exploiting vulnerabilities in these internet-exposed systems, such as authentication bypass and hardcoded credentials, to alter settings and potentially affect tank monitoring capabilities. The cyber activity is ongoing, with no specific threat actor named, although previous incidents have been linked to Iranian hackers, raising concerns about manipulation and potential safety risks.

2) Why it matters: These targeted cyberattacks on ATG systems are significant due to their implications for critical infrastructure. The tampering with tank monitoring systems in sectors like Energy and Transportation could lead to mismanagement of resources, undetected leaks, or equipment failures, posing safety hazards and economic consequences. Furthermore, the ability to exploit these systems without solid attribution complicates defensive measures, underscoring the persistent threat landscape critical infrastructures face.

3) What actions should be taken: Organizations utilizing ATG systems are encouraged to conduct thorough reviews of their cybersecurity postures, ensuring that systems are not internet-exposed and remote access is properly secured with firewalls and VPNs. Strengthening defenses through replacing default passwords, employing strong, unique credentials with multifactor authentication, updating system software, and actively monitoring for unauthorized changes can mitigate risks. By adopting these proactive measures, organizations can reduce vulnerabilities that could be exploited by threat actors.
