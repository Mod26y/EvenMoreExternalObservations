Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/cisa-warns-of-active-attacks-exploiting-android-linux-bugs/

1) What happened: CISA has issued a warning about active exploitation of vulnerabilities in the Android Framework and Linux kernel. The Android vulnerability, CVE-2025-48595, is a high-severity integer overflow flaw impacting Android versions 14 through 16, allowing attackers to gain increased privileges. The Linux vulnerability, CVE-2022-0492, involves a privilege escalation flaw in the cgroups v1 subsystem, used in containerized environments, granting attackers potential root-level access on host systems. Both vulnerabilities have been added to the Known Exploited Vulnerabilities (KEV) catalog and addressed in recent patches.

2) Why it matters: These vulnerabilities highlight potential security risks in widespread operating systems, threatening personal privacy and critical infrastructures reliant on Android and Linux systems. The ability to escalate privileges without user interaction suggests a significant danger that could be exploited in targeted attacks, potentially leading to unauthorized access, data breaches, or system compromise. Recognizing and mitigating such flaws is crucial for maintaining security posture and preventing attackers from leveraging these weaknesses within federal and broader network environments.

3) What actions should be taken: Organizations, especially those running Android or Linux systems, should prioritize patching these vulnerabilities with the specified security updates to mitigate the risk of exploitation. Security teams should review their systems to ensure compliance with directives like BOD 22-01 and consider implementing enhanced monitoring to detect and block potential exploits. Additionally, conducting regular security audits on containerized environments and narrowing the scope of elevated capabilities could further safeguard against privilege escalation. Engaging in proactive security measures, such as employing breach and attack simulation, can help in identifying and strengthening vulnerabilities in security defenses.
