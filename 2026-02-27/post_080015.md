Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/trend-micro-warns-of-critical-apex-one-rce-vulnerabilities/

Trend Micro identified and patched two critical vulnerabilities in its Apex One endpoint security platform that could lead to remote code execution. The flaws, CVE-2025-71210 and CVE-2025-71211, arise from path traversal issues in the management console, allowing unauthorized code execution on vulnerable systems. Immediate exposure primarily affects systems with externally accessible management consoles. Despite not currently being exploited in the wild, trend data indicates that Apex One has been a target in past cyberattacks.

This matters because these vulnerabilities could enable threat actors to execute unauthorized code, compromising system integrity and potentially leading to data breaches. The risk is heightened for organizations with management consoles accessible via the public internet. Given the history of previous Apex One exploitations and the sensitivity of data that endpoint security platforms manage, addressing these vulnerabilities is crucial for maintaining cybersecurity postures and protecting organizational assets.

Organizations using Apex One should promptly apply the latest patches provided by Trend Micro to ensure that these vulnerabilities are secured. Additionally, reviewing network configurations to restrict external access to the management console can further mitigate potential exploitation. Monitoring for any indicators of compromise associated with these vulnerabilities is recommended to swiftly respond to any unauthorized activities. Staying informed on updates and advisories from Trend Micro and CISA will aid in proactive defense strategies.
