Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/zoom-stealer-browser-extensions-harvest-corporate-meeting-intelligence/

The Zoom Stealer campaign targets 2.2 million Chrome, Firefox, and Microsoft Edge users via 18 browser extensions designed to collect meeting-related data from 28 video-conferencing platforms. The data harvested includes meeting URLs, IDs, passwords, and participant details, which are exfiltrated in real-time to the threat actor, DarkSpectre. This campaign, linked to a China-based group, exploits seemingly benign extensions to engage in corporate espionage and potentially undermine the confidentiality of online meetings.

This matters because it reveals significant vulnerabilities in how browser extensions can serve as conduits for espionage and unauthorized data collection. The harvested information can be used for corporate espionage, providing adversaries with a basis for impersonation and insider access to sensitive business meetings. Such a breach underscores the broader risk posed by seemingly benign software extensions and highlights the need for vigilance in digital security hygiene.

In light of this breach, organizations should advise users to regularly audit browser extensions for unnecessary permissions and remove those with excessive access rights to sensitive information. Security teams may also consider implementing browser security tools that monitor and control extension activity. Additionally, educating users on the importance of maintaining secure digital environments, such as using reputable extensions and verifying permissions, can help mitigate future risks.
