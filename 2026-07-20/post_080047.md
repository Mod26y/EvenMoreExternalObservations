Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/the-future-of-age-verification-your-face-never-leaves-your-device/

**What happened:**

The article discusses the evolving landscape of age verification laws, which are becoming more prevalent globally, and the privacy concerns they create. Traditional server-based facial age estimation methods, which require transmitting biometric data to external servers, are facing increasing scrutiny due to data breaches and privacy issues. In response, Incode Technologies has developed a new solution where age verification occurs entirely on users' devices, ensuring that facial data is neither transmitted nor stored externally. This on-device solution is coupled with a server-side analysis of session metadata to maintain security and prevent tampering.

**Why it matters:**

This development highlights the growing importance of privacy-centric solutions in the age verification domain. As laws mandating age checks expand, the approach to these checks must prioritize user privacy to maintain public trust and comply with regulations. The potential risks associated with server-based processes, including data breaches and identity theft, underscore the need for solutions like on-device verification, which offer robust protection against interception and misuse of biometric data. Moreover, increased methods of fraudulent activities, such as agentic fraud, require novel security measures to protect user identities effectively.

**What actions should be taken:**

Organizations involved in age verification should consider adopting privacy-preserving technologies like on-device age estimation to align with evolving regulatory requirements and user privacy expectations. Investing in local processing capabilities and minimizing data transfer can strengthen data protection efforts. Additionally, collaborating on anti-fraud measures without centralizing customer data could mitigate fraud risks while preserving privacy. It would be prudent for institutional stakeholders to monitor regulatory developments and update their systems to incorporate privacy-by-architecture principles, potentially consulting firms specializing in innovative privacy solutions to enhance their cybersecurity posture.
