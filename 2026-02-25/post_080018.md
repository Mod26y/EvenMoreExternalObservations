Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/phishing-campaign-targets-freight-and-logistics-orgs-in-the-us-europe/

In this article, a threat group known as "Diesel Vortex" has been executing a phishing campaign against freight and logistics companies in the US and Europe since September 2025. The group has stolen significant amounts of credentials by using 52 domains to target critical platforms and service providers. The campaign was discovered through an exposed repository containing SQL databases and Telegram communication logs, indicating the group's organized operations. This campaign is significant due to its scale and the fact that it targets a critical infrastructure sector often overlooked by enterprise security measures.

The significance of this campaign lies in its impact on the freight and logistics sector, a crucial component of global supply chains. By exploiting the cybersecurity vulnerabilities of its targets, Diesel Vortex could cause disruptions leading to economic and operational instability. Moreover, their sophisticated phishing tactics pose a high risk as they involve capturing sensitive information such as credentials, security tokens, and transaction data, which can further lead to fraudulent activities, including double brokering and cargo diversion.

Organizations impacted or operating within the freight and logistics sector should consider enhancing their cybersecurity awareness and training programs to mitigate phishing risks. Employing robust email filtering, implementing multi-factor authentication, and regularly updating incident response plans are critical steps towards bolstering defenses. Collaboration with cybersecurity providers and leveraging threat intelligence can help in identifying and neutralizing threats more promptly. Additionally, examining the full indicators of compromise (IoCs) provided in the report can aid in the proactive detection and mitigation of similar risks.
