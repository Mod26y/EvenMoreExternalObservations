Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/over-1-300-microsoft-sharepoint-servers-vulnerable-to-ongoing-attacks/

**What happened:** Over 1,300 Microsoft SharePoint servers remain exposed online and unpatched against a spoofing vulnerability, identified as CVE-2026-32201. This vulnerability allows unauthorized actors to conduct network spoofing through a flaw in input validation, enabling them to view and alter sensitive information. Microsoft acknowledged this vulnerability as a zero-day and issued patches during the April 2026 Patch Tuesday. Despite the availability of patches, the Internet security watchdog group Shadowserver noted that fewer than 200 systems have been patched to date.

**Why it matters:** This vulnerability poses significant cybersecurity risks as it allows attackers to breach confidentiality and integrity without user interaction. Given SharePoint's role in handling sensitive organizational data, the ongoing exploitation makes affected servers attractive targets for cyber espionage and data breaches. The continued exposure of these servers increases the risk of information theft, data manipulation, and potential operational impacts, especially concerning for federal agencies that use SharePoint servers extensively for document management and collaboration.

**What actions should be taken:** Organizations should immediately review their security posture and apply the available Microsoft patches for CVE-2026-32201. It's crucial that they ensure SharePoint servers are updated consistently to mitigate these risks. Additionally, organizations should reinforce their monitoring and incident response capabilities to detect and respond to potential exploitation attempts swiftly. For entities unable to implement the patches, following CISA's guidance to either apply vendor-recommended mitigations or discontinue the service temporarily might be prudent to safeguard their networks. Regular vulnerability assessments and employee training on cybersecurity best practices can further strengthen defenses.
