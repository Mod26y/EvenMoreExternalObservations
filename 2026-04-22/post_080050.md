**What Happened:** The article discusses a significant increase in the exploitation of Bomgar Remote Monitoring and Management (RMM) tools as a part of cyberattacks. Bomgar RMM tools, used for remote administration, have become a target due to vulnerabilities that allow attackers to gain unauthorized access to networks and sensitive data. This type of attack highlights vulnerabilities within the supply chain where trusted software tools are compromised to facilitate cyberattacks, affecting multiple organizations relying on these tools for legitimate purposes.

**Why It Matters:** The exploitation of Bomgar RMM tools is significant as it underscores the growing threat of supply chain attacks where third-party software becomes the entry point for attackers. Organizations become vulnerable not only through their direct security measures but also through the security posture of their service providers. This trend demonstrates the critical importance of assessing vendor and tool security, as an attack on commonly used software can lead to widespread data breaches and operational disruptions, affecting numerous businesses and public entities, including government agencies.

**Actions to Take:** Organizations should rigorously evaluate and monitor the security of their supply chain, particularly the software and tools provided by third parties. Implementing strict vendor management processes, regular security assessments, and timely patch management can mitigate risks related to RMM tools. Additionally, organizations may benefit from deploying network segmentation and enhanced monitoring of remote access tools to detect unusual activities promptly. Training staff on recognizing phishing attempts related to such tools can also diminish the chances of exploitation.
