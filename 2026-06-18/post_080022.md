Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/google-to-use-uk-and-eu-user-ip-addresses-for-ad-personalization/

1) What happened:

Google plans to incorporate users' IP addresses for ad measurement and personalization in the UK, EU Economic Area, and Switzerland by August 2026. This move signifies a shift in how IP addresses are used, moving from traffic routing and ad delivery to device identification for ad personalization. This requires user consent under regional laws like the GDPR, as IP addresses are considered personal data. Google will register under the IAB Europe's Transparency and Consent Framework to facilitate this change, although practical user consent mechanisms will only come into play later in the rollout.

2) Why it matters:

This change is significant because IP tracking in the EEA, UK, and Switzerland aligns with fingerprinting, a controversial method previously discouraged by Google. The plan may clash with upcoming regulatory guidance that seeks to tighten consent requirements for online advertising. It highlights the tension between targeted advertising and privacy standards in regions with stringent data protection laws. Google’s move sets a precedent that pressures advertisers to ensure compliance with these regulations and manage the shift's implications on privacy-aware ad practices.

3) What actions should be taken as a result of this information:

Organizations advertising in the affected regions should begin reviewing their consent management processes to align with the upcoming regulatory changes. This involves reinforcing mechanisms to obtain valid user consent for IP-based personalization. Companies may also consider consulting with legal experts to navigate the GDPR intricacies and ensure their practices do not constitute unauthorized fingerprinting. Practically, advertisers should educate users on their rights concerning ad personalization and offer transparent choices, possibly adopting Privacy-Enhancing Technologies (PETs) to safeguard user data. As regulations evolve, staying informed about new guidelines is crucial for maintaining compliance.
