Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/fortibleed-leak-exposes-fortinet-vpn-credentials-for-73-000-devices/

The "FortiBleed" data leak exposed credentials for Fortinet VPN devices tied to approximately 73,932 firewall URLs globally, impacting high-profile organizations such as Chevron, Samsung, and multiple governmental agencies. The data, gathered by a Russian-speaking threat group, included usernames, email addresses, and plaintext passwords for a variety of sectors including telecommunications, finance, healthcare, and government. The attackers used brute-force attacks and intercepted SSL VPN authentication hashes to acquire this data, potentially compromising internal networks through active directory environments.

The leak highlights significant vulnerabilities in VPN credential storage and management, showcasing the susceptibility of critical infrastructure to cyber threats. These compromised credentials could enable unauthorized access, leading to severe breaches, data theft, and operational disruption. The exposure of various sectors, including government and infrastructure operators, underscores the potential impact on national security and business continuity. Despite Fortinet's claims that the data stems from older incidents, the extensive nature of this leak confirms an ongoing risk needing immediate attention.

To mitigate risks, impacted organizations are advised to rotate all Fortinet VPN and associated administrative passwords immediately. Multi-factor authentication (MFA) should be enforced to add an extra layer of security. An internal review of gateway logs for suspicious activities can help identify potential breaches. It is essential to monitor and review employee credentials for any indications of exposure. Implementing regular security audits and adherence to cybersecurity best practices can minimize such vulnerabilities, reduce the attack surface, and protect organizational assets from unauthorized access.
