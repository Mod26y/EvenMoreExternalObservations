**What happened:** Although the specifics of the incident outlined in the article cannot be retrieved, the title suggests that the INC ransomware group has been successful by focusing on fundamental cybersecurity vulnerabilities. This implies they are likely exploiting common weaknesses such as outdated software, inadequate patch management, weak passwords, and lack of employee training. This approach allows them to infiltrate systems without relying on sophisticated, high-tech methods.

**Why it matters:** The success of INC ransomware by exploiting basic cybersecurity lapses highlights the critical need for organizations to strengthen their foundational security practices. It underscores that even well-established institutions are vulnerable if they neglect these essential security measures. This threat affects not only the integrity of organizational data but also public trust and financial stability, given the potential for costly breaches.

**What actions should be taken:** Organizations are encouraged to prioritize cybersecurity hygiene by ensuring regular updates and patching of systems, implementing strong password policies, and conducting regular security awareness training for employees. Additionally, investing in robust endpoint protection, consistent network monitoring, and regular vulnerability assessments can help mitigate the risks posed by such ransomware groups that thrive on basic security oversights.
