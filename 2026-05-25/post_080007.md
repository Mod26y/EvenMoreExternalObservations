Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/ghost-cms-sql-injection-flaw-exploited-in-large-scale-clickfix-campaign/

The article outlines a cybersecurity incident involving the exploitation of a critical SQL injection vulnerability, CVE-2026-26980, in Ghost CMS by a large-scale campaign named ClickFix. Threat actors injected malicious JavaScript into over 700 websites, retrieving admin API keys to gain management access. High-profile sites such as those of Harvard and Oxford Universities were impacted. Unpatched systems, despite a fix being available since February 19, have been repeatedly targeted due to their vulnerability.

This incident is significant because it highlights the rampant threat of SQL injection vulnerabilities, emphasizing the importance of timely patch management. The campaign affects numerous high-profile institutions, underscoring the potential for widespread data breaches and manipulation of digital content. The attack's capability to plant instructional lures through authentic-seeming interfaces like fake Cloudflare prompts further increases the risk to unsuspecting users.

In response, Ghost CMS administrators should ensure they update to version 6.19.1 or later, rotating all API keys since previous ones may be compromised. It is crucial to conduct a thorough review of sites for injected scripts, utilizing provided IoCs for guidance. Maintaining a log of admin API calls for 30 days will aid in retrospective analyses. Enhanced vigilance and proactive patch management practices should be promoted to safeguard against similar vulnerabilities.
