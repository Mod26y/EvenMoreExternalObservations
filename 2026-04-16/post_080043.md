Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/signed-software-abused-to-deploy-antivirus-killing-scripts/

The article outlines a cybersecurity incident involving a digitally signed adware tool used to distribute scripts that deactivate antivirus protections on numerous endpoints across multiple sectors, including government, healthcare, and education. More than 23,500 infected hosts in 124 countries were detected in one day, with hundreds within high-value networks. The adware, signed by Dragon Boss Solutions LLC, was purportedly innocuous but leveraged advanced update mechanisms to deploy antivirus-killing payloads using elevated privileges.

This matters because the deployment of antivirus-disabling scripts via signed software indicates a sophisticated attack vector that undermines trust in digitally signed software. The potential for this infrastructure to introduce more dangerous payloads represents a significant threat to critical sectors, including municipal and educational institutions, healthcare, and infrastructure providers. It highlights vulnerabilities in existing security protocols, emphasizing the need for vigilance in monitoring, detecting, and responding to such stealthy cyber threats.

As a response, organizations are advised to proactively monitor for indicators of compromise, such as specific WMI event subscriptions, scheduled tasks, and processes associated with Dragon Boss Solutions. Reviewing and potentially cleansing affected systems of modifications to the hosts file or antivirus exclusions is essential. Additionally, ensuring domain registration of critical update domains to prevent malicious actors from exploiting these for further distribution is advised. Enhanced monitoring for unusual network communication, particularly to domains used in the campaign, can aid early detection and mitigation.
