1) The emergence of quantum computing, often referred to as "Q-Day," signifies a future point when quantum computers will be able to break current cryptographic systems. This potential quantum leap threatens to render commonly used encryption methods, such as RSA and ECC, obsolete. The article discusses the imperative for organizations to begin preparing for this paradigm shift in data security to mitigate potential risks associated with quantum-powered attacks.

2) The significance of "Q-Day" lies in its capacity to disrupt global cybersecurity frameworks. If current cryptographic practices become vulnerable to quantum decryption, the confidentiality and integrity of sensitive data across various sectors could be at risk. This includes critical infrastructure, financial systems, and governmental operations, making it crucial for organizations to proactively address quantum risks to safeguard their information assets in a post-quantum era.

3) To effectively prepare for "Q-Day," entities should consider conducting comprehensive quantum risk assessments to identify potential vulnerabilities in their current encryption methods. Investing in research and development of quantum-resistant algorithms, such as lattice-based cryptography, can bolster defenses against future threats. Transition planning, including a roadmap for adopting quantum-safe encryption technologies, is advised to ensure a seamless migration when quantum computing becomes a reality. Engaging with industry standards groups may also facilitate staying updated on best practices and innovations in post-quantum cryptography.
