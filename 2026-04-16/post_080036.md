Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/wordpress-plugin-suite-hacked-to-push-malware-to-thousands-of-sites/

The article reports a security breach in over 30 WordPress plugins within the EssentialPlugin suite. This suite had malicious code inserted through a backdoor after a change in ownership in 2025, affecting numerous websites. Recently, this malware became active, causing sites to generate spam pages and redirection by leveraging command-and-control servers. Disguised to remain undetected by site owners, the malware executed spammy activities only apparent to search engine bots.

This security incident is significant as it reflects the vulnerabilities within popular web development tools relied upon by many sites for functionality enhancements. Such breaches can undermine the credibility of involved web services and expose sites to reputational damage, search engine penalties, and a potential loss of revenue due to the trust deficit in users’ minds.

In response to this information, administrators should diligently monitor their site's plugins for suspicious activity and verify all updates before implementation. It is advised to review and clean configurations like wp-config.php to ensure the malware hasn't persisted. Organizations could consider conducting regular security audits to ensure the integrity of their tools and systems, considering alternate cybersecurity measures or hosting services with stringent security protocols.
