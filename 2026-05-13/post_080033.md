Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/signal-adds-security-warnings-for-social-engineering-phishing-attacks/

1) Signal recently implemented new in-app security features to combat social engineering and phishing attacks. The enhancements aim to provide users with confirmations and warnings against fraudulent messages, particularly targeting high-profile users. Russian state-sponsored hackers were found exploiting Signal's Linked Device feature by deceiving victims into sharing QR or one-time codes, thereby gaining unauthorized access to accounts. Signal highlights unverified contacts and reminds users to reject requests for registration codes or PINs. The overall purpose is to increase user awareness and create intentional friction to avoid inadvertent engagement with malicious entities.

2) This development is significant because it addresses the threat of social engineering attacks, a common and effective method for cyber adversaries to bypass technical defenses. High-profile users and organizations are particularly at risk, potentially leading to significant data breaches and unauthorized data access. These attacks can severely compromise sensitive information and privacy, causing reputational damage, financial losses, and endangering personal security. By improving user education and providing real-time alerts, Signal enhances its user's ability to detect and thwart fraudulent attempts, thus strengthening overall cybersecurity resilience.

3) In light of this information, organizations should prioritize employee education on recognizing social engineering and phishing attempts and encourage vigilant scrutiny of unsolicited requests. Regular training sessions could be beneficial to reinforce best practices, such as verifying unknown contact credentials. Additionally, institutions should consider reviewing and updating their incident response strategies to include protocols for managing linked devices and facilitating the prompt removal of unauthorized connections. By implementing these steps, the organization can improve its cybersecurity posture and contribute to mitigating the risks associated with social engineering threats.
