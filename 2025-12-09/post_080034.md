Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/fincen-says-ransomware-gangs-extorted-over-21b-from-2022-to-2024/

1) The Financial Crimes Enforcement Network (FinCEN) reported that ransomware groups extracted over $2.1 billion from 2022 to 2024, with activity peaking in 2023. During this period, key groups like ALPHV/BlackCat and LockBit were highly active, leading to numerous extortion incidents, but the number of attacks and financial impact decreased in 2024 following significant law enforcement interventions. Manufacturing, financial services, and healthcare sectors were notably targeted, with financial services suffering the most financial losses. The analysis identified 267 distinct ransomware families, with Bitcoin being the prevalent payment method.

2) The FinCEN findings underscore the severe financial impact ransomware can have on critical industries, emphasizing the importance of improved cybersecurity measures and law enforcement efforts. The data highlights the sophistication and adaptation of cybercriminal tactics, as well as the necessity for continuous monitoring and collaboration between organizations and authorities. Tracking the use of cryptocurrencies is crucial for disrupting illicit payments. The report's insights into high-risk sectors guide resource allocation and policy emphasis, reinforcing the need for proactive cyber defense and response strategies.

3) In response to the report, organizations should enhance their cybersecurity infrastructure by investing in robust protection, detection, and response measures. Regular training and awareness programs are crucial to educate employees and stakeholders on the complexities of ransomware threats and safe practices. Collaboration with authorities like the FBI and FinCEN can improve incident response and threat intelligence sharing. Organizations should consider diversifying their transaction methods to mitigate the risk of ransomware negotiations, focusing particularly on reducing exposure to commonly exploited vulnerabilities in their financial systems.
