Original Article: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/traffic-violation-scams-switch-to-qr-codes-in-new-phishing-texts/

1) Scammers have shifted tactics to disseminate fake traffic violation notices via text messages that include a QR code, leading recipients to phishing sites. These messages impersonate state courts across the U.S., demanding a small payment of $6.99. Scanning the QR code initiates a sequence to capture personal and financial information under the guise of resolving a supposed pending violation. This campaign has spread to multiple states, including New York, California, and Texas, posing as communications from either local courts or the Department of Motor Vehicles.

2) The shift toward QR codes in phishing scams highlights evolving cyber threat tactics, making it easier for fraudsters to bypass traditional security measures and exploit the trust and urgency implicit in legal notices. The scam’s focus on widely utilized communication methods and small, seemingly innocuous payment demands increases the probability of victims complying without scrutiny. This is significant because it can lead to identity theft, fraudulent financial activities, and other cybercrimes, impacting individuals’ privacy and financial security.

3) To mitigate these scams, individuals and organizations should be vigilant about communications requesting payments through unfamiliar channels, like unverified QR codes. Raising public awareness about these scams, including recognition and verification methods for official documents, can deter victim compliance. Organizations can provide guidance on how to authenticate communications from legitimate agencies and suggest ignoring unsolicited messages requesting personal information. Additionally, investing in security awareness training and implementing robust email and text filtering techniques can reduce exposure to such phishing attempts.
